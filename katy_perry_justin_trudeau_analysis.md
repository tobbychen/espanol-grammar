# Analysis of the Article on Katy Perry and Justin Trudeau

## English Translation

Katy Perry and Justin Trudeau go out in Paris to celebrate Katy's 41st birthday. They see a show at the Crazy Horse. It is the first time they are seen together as a couple.

A few weeks earlier, Katy talks about her new relationship during her London concert. In that concert, a fan asks Katy to marry him.

People start talking about Katy and Justin in July. This happens just after Katy separates from Orlando Bloom. Justin leaves his wife in 2023. Later, Katy and Justin are together in Montreal and celebrate her birthday in Paris.

## Grammatical Analysis

Here is a grammatical breakdown of key sentences from the article:

*   **`Katy Perry y Justin Trudeau salen por París para celebrar el 41 cumpleaños de Katy.`**
    *   **`salen por París`**: `salen` is the third-person plural, present tense of `salir` (to go out). The preposition `por` indicates movement through or around a place ("around Paris").
    *   **`para celebrar`**: The structure `para` + infinitive (`celebrar`) is used to express purpose, meaning "in order to celebrate."

*   **`Es la primera vez que se les ve juntos como pareja.`**
    *   **`se les ve`**: This is a passive construction using `se`. `les` is the indirect object pronoun for "them." The literal translation is "one sees them," but it is more naturally rendered as "they are seen."

*   **`En ese concierto, un fan le pide a Katy que se case con él.`**
    *   **`le pide a Katy`**: The indirect object pronoun `le` (to her) is included for emphasis and is clarified by the phrase `a Katy`.
    *   **`que se case`**: `que` introduces a subordinate clause. `se case` is the present subjunctive form of the reflexive verb `casarse` (to marry). The subjunctive is required after a verb of request like `pedir que...` (to ask that...).

*   **`La gente empieza a hablar de Katy y Justin en julio.`**
    *   **`empieza a hablar`**: This is a verbal periphrasis (`empezar a` + infinitive) that means "to start to do something."

*   **`Esto ocurre justo después de que Katy se separe de Orlando Bloom.`**
    *   **`justo después de que`**: A conjunction phrase meaning "right after."
    *   **`se separe`**: The verb `separarse` (to separate) is in the present subjunctive (`separe`). The subjunctive is often used after conjunctions of time like `después de que` when the action is subsequent to the main clause's action.

*   **`Justin deja a su mujer en 2023.`**
    *   **`deja a su mujer`**: The verb `dejar` means "to leave." The "personal a" is used before `su mujer` (his wife) because the direct object is a specific person.
