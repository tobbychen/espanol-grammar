# Meta New Mexico Verdict Child Safety Lawsuit Analysis (ES-EN)

## Abstract

This article reports that a New Mexico jury delivered a major legal defeat to Meta over the safety of its platforms. The case centers on accusations that Meta misled parents and users, failed to protect minors online, and prioritized profit over child safety. The text also notes Meta's intention to appeal and places the ruling within a broader wave of lawsuits against social media companies over addictive design and harm to teenagers' mental health.

## Raw Spanish Text

Un jurado de Nuevo México ha asestado una importante derrota judicial a Meta, la empresa propietaria de Facebook, Instagram y WhatsApp. El fiscal general del estado acusó a Meta de engañar a padres y usuarios sobre la seguridad de sus plataformas.

La demanda alegaba que Meta facilitaba la explotación infantil y no protegía a los jóvenes en Internet. El jurado condenó a Meta a pagar 375 millones de dólares en multas. El fiscal general calificó el veredicto de victoria histórica y afirmó que ninguna empresa tecnológica está por encima de la ley. Argumentó que Meta anteponía los beneficios a la seguridad de los niños.

Meta, sin embargo, discrepa totalmente de la decisión y tiene previsto recurrir. La empresa afirma que ha realizado grandes esfuerzos para mantener la seguridad de sus plataformas. Este caso forma parte de una tendencia más amplia, ya que Meta se enfrenta a miles de demandas más. Muchas de ellas acusan a las empresas de redes sociales de crear productos adictivos que perjudican la salud mental de los adolescentes. En mayo comenzará la segunda parte de este juicio para decidir si Meta debe cambiar sus sistemas.

## English Translation

A New Mexico jury has dealt Meta, the company that owns Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp, a major legal defeat. The state's attorney general accused Meta of misleading parents and users about the safety of its platforms.

The lawsuit alleged that Meta enabled child exploitation and did not protect young people on the internet. The jury ordered Meta to pay 375 million dollars in fines. The attorney general described the verdict as a historic victory and stated that no technology company is above the law. He argued that Meta was putting profits before the safety of children.

Meta, however, completely disagrees with the decision and plans to appeal. The company says that it has made major efforts to keep its platforms safe. This case is part of a broader trend, since Meta is facing thousands of additional lawsuits. Many of them accuse social media companies of creating addictive products that harm teenagers' mental health. In May, the second part of this trial will begin to decide whether Meta must change its systems.

## Grammar Analysis (Key Structures)

1. `ha asestado una importante derrota judicial a Meta`
- Present perfect: `ha asestado` reports a recent action with present relevance.
- `asestar una derrota / un golpe` is a formal journalistic expression meaning "to deal a defeat/blow".

2. `la empresa propietaria de Facebook, Instagram y WhatsApp`
- Appositional noun phrase adds identifying information about `Meta`.
- `propietaria de` = "owner of".

3. `acusó a Meta de engañar a padres y usuarios`
- `acusar a alguien de + infinitive` = "to accuse someone of doing something".
- `a Meta` uses the personal `a`.

4. `La demanda alegaba que...`
- Imperfect `alegaba` presents the claim as part of the legal background of the case.
- `alegar que` = "to allege that".

5. `facilitaba la explotación infantil y no protegía a los jóvenes`
- Imperfect verbs describe ongoing or repeated alleged conduct in the past.
- The two verbs are coordinated to present parallel accusations.

6. `condenó a Meta a pagar...`
- `condenar a alguien a + infinitive` = "to sentence/order someone to do something".
- Preterite `condenó` reports the completed ruling.

7. `calificó el veredicto de victoria histórica`
- `calificar algo de + noun/adjective` = "to describe something as..."
- Another completed reporting verb in the preterite.

8. `ninguna empresa tecnológica está por encima de la ley`
- `ninguna` gives negative emphasis: "no ...".
- `estar por encima de la ley` is a fixed expression meaning "to be above the law".

9. `Meta anteponía los beneficios a la seguridad de los niños`
- Imperfect `anteponía` suggests an ongoing priority pattern in the accusation.
- `anteponer A a B` = "to put A before B".

10. `discrepa totalmente de la decisión`
- Present tense shows Meta's current position.
- `discrepar de` = "to disagree with".

11. `tiene previsto recurrir`
- Fixed structure: `tener previsto + infinitive` = "to plan/intend to..."
- `recurrir` in legal context means "to appeal".

12. `ha realizado grandes esfuerzos para mantener...`
- Present perfect for recent efforts still relevant now.
- `para + infinitive` expresses purpose.

13. `forma parte de una tendencia más amplia`
- Fixed expression: `formar parte de` = "to be part of".

14. `se enfrenta a miles de demandas`
- Pronominal verb: `enfrentarse a` = "to face".
- It often introduces legal, political, or practical challenges.

15. `productos adictivos que perjudican la salud mental`
- Relative clause `que perjudican...` modifies `productos adictivos`.
- `perjudicar` means "to harm" or "to damage".

16. `para decidir si Meta debe cambiar sus sistemas`
- `si` introduces an indirect yes/no question.
- `debe cambiar` expresses obligation or necessity.
