**Raw Text:** Los defensores de esta energía afirman que los largos días de verano en el norte proporcionan una fuerte luz solar, lo que hace que la tecnología solar sea útil, aunque los inviernos sean oscuros.

- `defensores`: proponents, defenders
- `proporcionan`: they provide
- `lo que`: which (referring to a whole idea)
- `aunque`: although, even though
- `sean`: they are (subjunctive)

## Set Phrases and constructions

- **`bajo cero`**: Literally "below zero," this is the standard phrase for expressing negative temperatures.
- **`están probando`**: The Present Progressive (`estar` + gerund) is used to emphasize an action happening "right now" or over the current period.
- **`lo que hace que + [subjunctive]`**: A common and important structure. `lo que` ("which" or "the thing that") refers to a whole concept, and when it's the subject of a verb like `hacer que` or `provocar que` (to cause), it triggers the subjunctive mood (`sea`).
- **`aunque + [subjunctive]`**: `aunque` means "although" or "even if." It's used with the subjunctive (`sean`) to introduce a potential obstacle or conceded fact that does not invalidate the main point ("solar is useful *even though* the winters may be dark").
- **`en comparación con`**: "in comparison with." A standard phrase for making comparisons.
- **`debido a que`**: "because" or "due to the fact that." Used to provide a reason.
- **`cualquier lugar`**: "any place." The adjective `cualquier` is the shortened form of `cualquiera` used before a noun.
