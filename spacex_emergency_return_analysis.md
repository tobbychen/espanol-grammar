## Raw Spanish Text

Una cápsula de SpaceX abandonó la Estación Espacial Internacional antes de lo previsto porque uno de los astronautas necesitaba atención médica urgente. La tripulación estaba formada por dos estadounidenses, un astronauta japonés y un cosmonauta ruso.

La NASA no reveló detalles sobre el estado de salud para proteger la privacidad. La cápsula inició su viaje de regreso a la Tierra después de más de cinco meses en el espacio. La nave espacial comenzó su regreso por la tarde y se dirigió hacia el océano Pacífico, cerca de California. Se esperaba que el viaje durara unas diez horas e incluyera una entrada muy caliente a través de la atmósfera terrestre. Las imágenes en directo mostraban cómo la cápsula se alejaba lentamente de la estación espacial mientras la tripulación permanecía sentada en su interior con sus trajes espaciales.

La NASA dijo que era la primera vez que terminaba una misión en la estación espacial antes de tiempo debido a un problema de salud. Las autoridades explicaron que los médicos en la Tierra pueden proporcionar una mejor atención y realizar mejores controles. El comandante de la misión dijo que todos estaban estables, pero que el regreso era la mejor opción. Otra tripulación llegará a la estación más tarde.

## English Translation

A SpaceX capsule left the International Space Station earlier than planned because one of the astronauts needed urgent medical attention. The crew consisted of two Americans, a Japanese astronaut, and a Russian cosmonaut.

NASA did not reveal details about the health condition in order to protect privacy. The capsule began its return trip to Earth after more than five months in space. The spacecraft started its return in the afternoon and headed towards the Pacific Ocean, near California. It was expected that the trip would last about ten hours and include a very hot entry through the Earth's atmosphere. Live images showed the capsule slowly moving away from the space station while the crew remained seated inside in their spacesuits.

NASA said it was the first time a mission on the space station had ended early due to a health problem. Authorities explained that doctors on Earth can provide better care and perform better checks. The mission commander said that everyone was stable, but that returning was the best option. Another crew will arrive at the station later.

## Grammatical Analysis

1.  **Preterite vs. Imperfect Tense:** This text is an excellent example of how both tenses work together to tell a story.
    *   **Preterite**: Used for the main, completed actions that drive the narrative forward: `abandonó` (it left), `inició` (it began), `se dirigió` (it headed), `dijo` (said), `explicaron` (explained).
    *   **Imperfect**: Used for background information, descriptions of ongoing scenes, and states of being: `necesitaba` (needed - the state that caused the action), `estaba formada` (consisted of - a description of the crew), `mostraban` (were showing - describing what the live feed was doing), `se alejaba` (was moving away - an ongoing action in the scene), `permanecía` (remained - describing the state of the crew).

2.  **Imperfect Subjunctive:**
    *   `Se esperaba que el viaje **durara**...` (It was expected that the trip **would last**...). The impersonal expression of expectation in the past (`se esperaba que...`) triggers the use of the imperfect subjunctive (`durara`) to describe what was expected to happen.

3.  **Future Tense:**
    *   `Otra tripulación **llegará**...` (Another crew **will arrive**...). This is a clear use of the simple future tense to state a future event.

4.  **Past Participles as Adjectives:**
    *   `La tripulación estaba **formada** por...` (The crew was **made up** of...). Here, `formada` is a past participle used with `estar` to describe the crew's composition.
    *   `...la tripulación permanecía **sentada**...` (...the crew remained **seated**...). Here, `sentada` acts as an adjective describing the state of the crew.

## Idioms and Expressions

1.  **Antes de lo previsto:**
    *   **Meaning:** "Earlier than planned" or "earlier than expected." A common phrase for something happening ahead of schedule.

2.  **Viaje de regreso:**
    *   **Meaning:** "Return trip." A standard compound noun phrase.

3.  **En directo:**
    *   **Meaning:** "Live," as in a live television or internet broadcast.

4.  **Antes de tiempo:**
    *   **Meaning:** "Early" or "ahead of time." It can also sometimes imply "prematurely." It is similar to `antes de lo previsto`.

5.  **Mejor opción:**
    *   **Meaning:** "Best option."
