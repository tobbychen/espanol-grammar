# Analysis of Adolescent Sleep Deprivation Article

## Raw Spanish Text

La falta de sueño entre los adolescentes estadounidenses es una epidemia, ya que sólo uno de cada diez duerme las 8 a 10 horas recomendadas por noche.

El principal factor causante de esto son las políticas públicas, en concreto los horarios tempranos de entrada a la escuela, que obligan a los adolescentes a despertarse horas antes de que su reloj biológico natural esté preparado, debido a un retraso en la liberación de la hormona melatonina en torno a la pubertad.

Esta falta crónica de sueño afecta gravemente al cuerpo y al cerebro de los adolescentes. Les roba el sueño esencial para el aprendizaje, la consolidación de la memoria y el procesamiento emocional. Las consecuencias son amplias, imitando los síntomas del TDAH, la irritabilidad y contribuyendo significativamente a problemas de salud mental como el aumento de la tristeza, la desesperanza e incluso los intentos de suicidio. También aumentan los riesgos para la salud física, como la obesidad y la diabetes.

Los investigadores sostienen que retrasar la hora de inicio de las clases a no antes de las 8.30 de la mañana es la solución inequívoca. Los distritos que han hecho este cambio informan de poderosos beneficios, como mejores resultados en los exámenes, menor absentismo escolar, mejor salud mental y física, y comunidades más seguras debido a un fuerte descenso de las tasas de accidentes de coche entre los adolescentes.

## English Translation

The lack of sleep among American teenagers is an epidemic, as only one in ten gets the recommended 8 to 10 hours per night.

The main causal factor for this is public policy, specifically early school start times, which force adolescents to wake up hours before their natural biological clock is ready, due to a delay in the release of the hormone melatonin around puberty.

This chronic lack of sleep severely affects the body and brain of adolescents. It robs them of the sleep essential for learning, memory consolidation, and emotional processing. The consequences are broad, imitating the symptoms of ADHD, irritability, and contributing significantly to mental health problems such as increased sadness, hopelessness, and even suicide attempts. The risks to physical health, such as obesity and diabetes, also increase.

Researchers argue that delaying the start time of classes to no earlier than 8:30 a.m. is the unequivocal solution. Districts that have made this change report powerful benefits, such as better test scores, lower school absenteeism, improved mental and physical health, and safer communities due to a sharp decline in the rates of car accidents among teenagers.

## Grammatical Analysis

*   **Present Tense Dominance**: The text predominantly uses the simple present tense (e.g., `es`, `duerme`, `son`, `obligan`, `afecta`, `roba`, `sostienen`) to state facts, general truths, and research findings.
*   **Ser vs. Estar**: The verb `ser` is used for definitions and characteristics (`es una epidemia`, `son las políticas`), while `estar` is used for states or conditions (`esté preparado`).
*   **Subjunctive Mood**: A key example is `...antes de que su reloj biológico natural **esté** preparado`. The subjunctive `esté` is required after the conjunction `antes de que` (before) when the main clause's action precedes the subordinate clause's action.
*   **Reflexive Verbs**: `despertarse` (to wake up) is a reflexive verb, where the action is performed by the subject on itself. The `se` pronoun indicates this reflexivity.
*   **Gerunds**: `imitando` (imitating) and `contribuyendo` (contributing) are used as gerunds (present participles). They function adverbially to modify the main action, describing the consequences of sleep deprivation.
*   **Connectors and Phrases**: The text uses various connectors to build its argument:
    *   `ya que` (since, because)
    *   `en concreto` (specifically)
    *   `debido a` (due to)
    *   `como` (such as, like)
*   **Passive Voice with "se"**: While not explicitly used here, it's worth noting that Spanish often uses a reflexive `se` construction for passive voice (e.g., `se dice que...` - it is said that...). This text, however, favors a direct, active voice.
*   **Adjectives**: Adjectives typically follow the nouns they modify (e.g., `falta **crónica**`, `reloj biológico **natural**`, `solución **inequívoca**`).
