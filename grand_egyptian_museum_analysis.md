### Raw Spanish Article

El Gran Museo Egipcio se ha inaugurado oficialmente cerca de las pirámides de Guiza tras 20 años de construcción. Costó alrededor de mil millones de dólares, financiados en su mayor parte mediante préstamos japoneses.

El nuevo museo, uno de los mayores del mundo dedicados a una sola civilización, alberga decenas de miles de artefactos, incluidos todos los tesoros del antiguo faraón Tutankamón, mostrados juntos por primera vez. Presidentes, primeros ministros y miembros de la realeza asistieron a la gran inauguración, que contó con fuegos artificiales y espectáculos de drones que iluminaron el cielo nocturno de El Cairo.

El gobierno egipcio espera que el museo impulse el turismo y ayude a la economía del país, que atraviesa dificultades. A pesar de retos como la Primavera Árabe, la pandemia y los conflictos regionales, Egipto celebra por fin un nuevo capítulo de su larga historia, con el objetivo de atraer a millones de visitantes en los próximos años. Construido cerca de las Grandes Pirámides, el museo simboliza el orgullo nacional y el patrimonio cultural.

### English Analysis

The Grand Egyptian Museum has officially opened near the Giza pyramids after 20 years of construction. It cost approximately one billion dollars, financed mostly through Japanese loans.

The new museum, one of the world's largest dedicated to a single civilization, houses tens of thousands of artifacts. This includes all the treasures of the ancient pharaoh Tutankhamun, which are being displayed together for the first time. The grand opening was attended by presidents, prime ministers, and royalty, and it featured fireworks and drone shows that lit up the Cairo night sky.

The Egyptian government hopes the museum will boost tourism and help the country's struggling economy. Despite challenges like the Arab Spring, the pandemic, and regional conflicts, Egypt is finally celebrating a new chapter in its long history. The goal is to attract millions of visitors in the coming years. Built near the Great Pyramids, the museum symbolizes national pride and cultural heritage.

### Grammar Points

*   **Reflexive Passive Voice (`se` + verb):** The article uses `se ha inaugurado` (has been inaugurated). This construction is common in Spanish to describe an action without specifying who performed it.
*   **Past Tenses:**
    *   **Present Perfect (`haber` + past participle):** `se ha inaugurado` is used for a recent event with present relevance.
    *   **Preterite:** Used for completed past actions: `costó` (it cost), `asistieron` (they attended), `contó` (it featured).
*   **Subjunctive Mood:** The subjunctive is used after expressions of hope or desire.
    *   `...espera que el museo **impulse** el turismo y **ayude** a la economía...` (...hopes that the museum **boosts** tourism and **helps** the economy...). Both `impulse` and `ayude` are in the present subjunctive.
*   **Adjectival Past Participles:** Past participles are used like adjectives to describe nouns. They must agree in gender and number.
    *   `financiados` (financed) agrees with `préstamos` (loans).
    *   `dedicados` (dedicated) agrees with `museos` (implied in `uno de los mayores`).
    *   `mostrados` (shown) agrees with `tesoros` (treasures).
    *   `Construido` (Built) agrees with `el museo`.
*   **Prepositional Phrases:**
    *   `cerca de`: near
    *   `tras`: after
    *   `a pesar de`: despite / in spite of
    *   `mediante`: through / by means of
