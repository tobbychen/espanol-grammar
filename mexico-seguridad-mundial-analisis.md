# Analysis of Mexico World Cup Security Spending

## 1. Original Spanish Text

México está gastando mucho dinero en seguridad para prepararse para el Mundial de fútbol de junio.

En el estado de Jalisco, el gobierno está utilizando miles de agentes de policía, nueva tecnología y helicópteros para proteger a los equipos de fútbol y a los aficionados. Han gastado 55 millones de dólares en nuevo equipamiento para demostrar al mundo que la zona es segura.

Sin embargo, muchas familias locales están muy enfadadas por esto. Dicen que el gobierno se está centrando en un evento deportivo mientras ignora una crisis enorme: más de 16 000 personas están desaparecidas o han sido asesinadas en esta región. Las familias de los desaparecidos han formado grupos para buscar a sus seres queridos por su cuenta. A menudo excavan en zonas abandonadas en busca de cadáveres porque dicen que la policía no les ayuda lo suficiente. Estas familias suelen trabajar sin ningún tipo de protección, a pesar de que las zonas pueden ser peligrosas. A solo unos kilómetros de donde arriverán los aficionados para los partidos, estos grupos encontraron recientemente bolsas con huesos humanos.

Mientras el gobierno dice que está luchando contra los grupos criminales, el número de personas desaparecidas sigue aumentando. Las familias sienten que el despliegue de fuerzas para el Mundial es solo para aparentar y no protege a los ciudadanos de México.

## 2. English Translation

Mexico is spending a lot of money on security to prepare for the June World Cup football tournament.

In the state of Jalisco, the government is using thousands of police officers, new technology, and helicopters to protect football teams and fans. They have spent 55 million dollars on new equipment to show the world that the area is safe.

However, many local families are very angry about this. They say the government is focusing on a sporting event while ignoring an enormous crisis: more than 16,000 people are missing or have been murdered in this region. Families of the missing have formed groups to search for their loved ones on their own. They often dig in abandoned areas looking for corpses because they say the police don't help them enough. These families usually work without any protection, even though the areas can be dangerous. Just a few kilometers from where fans will arrive for the matches, these groups recently found bags with human bones.

While the government says it is fighting criminal groups, the number of missing people continues to increase. The families feel that the deployment of forces for the World Cup is just for show and does not protect the citizens of Mexico.

## 4. Grammar Notes

### Present Progressive (Estar + gerund)
- *está gastando* — is spending
- *está utilizando* — is using
- *está centrando* — is focusing (centrarse)
- *sigue aumentando* — continues to increase (seguir + gerund)

### Present Perfect
- *Han gastado 55 millones* — They have spent 55 million
- *han formado grupos* — they have formed groups
- *han sido asesinadas* — have been murdered (passive perfect)

### Passive Voice (Ser + past participle)
- *han sido asesinados* — have been murdered
- The agent is not mentioned but would use "por" + agent

### "Por su cuenta" — On their own
- *buscar a sus seres queridos por su cuenta* — to search for their loved ones on their own

### "A pesar de / a pesar de que" — Despite/Although
- *a pesar de que las zonas pueden ser peligrosas* — even though the areas can be dangerous

### Present Subjunctive in Noun Clauses
- *esperan que el gobierno haga algo* — they hope the government will do something
- (subjunctive after expressions of hope/want/doubt when subject changes)

### "A menudo" — Often
- Adverbial phrase placed before or after verb

### "A solo unos kilómetros de donde" — Just a few kilometers from where
- *a + number + de + location* = at a distance of
- *de donde* = from where / the place where

### "Para + infinitive" — Purpose
- *para protegera los equipos* — to protect the teams (purpose)
- *para demostr ar al mundo* — to show the world

### "Sentir que + indicative"
- *Las familias sienten que el despliegue... es solo para aparentar*
- When "sentir" (to feel/believe) introduces a factual belief, indicative is used (not subjunctive)

### "Seguir + gerund" — To continue doing
- *el número sigue aumentando* — the number continues to increase
- *siguen buscando* — they continue searching

## 6. Cultural Context

**Jalisco** — A Mexican state known for its capital Guadalajara. It has been affected by drug cartel violence and disappearances.

**Missing persons crisis in Mexico** — Mexico has one of the highest numbers of missing persons in the world, with over 100,000 people reported missing overall. Families often take it upon themselves to search for loved ones because official investigations are perceived as inadequate.

**World Cup context** — Mexico hosted the 2026 FIFA World Cup (jointly with USA and Canada). Significant security investments were made to protect teams, fans, and project an image of safety.

**The tension** — The article highlights the disparity between huge security spending for a sporting event versus the ongoing crisis of disappearances that affects ordinary citizens.
