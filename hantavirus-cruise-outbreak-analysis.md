# Analysis of Hantavirus Outbreak on Cruise Ship

## 1. Original Spanish Text

Se ha producido un brote de una peligrosa enfermedad llamada hantavirus a bordo del Crucero MV Hondius en el océano Atlántico.

Hasta ahora han fallecido tres personas: un matrimonio de los Países Bajos y una persona de Alemania. Además, un hombre británico se encuentra muy grave y está siendo tratado en una unidad de cuidados intensivos en Sudáfrica.

La Organización Mundial de la Salud (OMS) afirma que hay al menos siete casos en total. Las autoridades locales de África Occidental están preocupadas y no han permitido que los 149 pasajeros bajen del barco porque temen que la enfermedad se propague entre la población local. Es posible que solo se permita bajar a dos tripulantes enfermos para que reciban asistencia médica. El resto de los pasajeros podría tener que navegar ocho días más para llegar a las Islas Canarias.

El hantavirus lo transmiten roedores como ratas y ratones. Los humanos pueden enfermarse si tocan los excrementos, la saliva o la orina de estos animales. Los expertos creen que los pasajeros podrían haberlo contraído durante excursiones de observación de aves en tierra. Un gran problema es que pueden pasar hasta ocho semanas hasta que una persona muestre síntomas, por lo que las autoridades deben vigilar a los pasajeros durante mucho tiempo. La gente a bordo del barco está muy preocupada porque sienten que no tienen suficiente información y están atrapados en el barco.

## 2. English Translation

An outbreak of a dangerous disease called hantavirus has occurred aboard the cruise ship MV Hondius in the Atlantic Ocean.

So far, three people have died: a married couple from the Netherlands and a person from Germany. Additionally, a British man is in critical condition and is being treated in an intensive care unit in South Africa.

The World Health Organization (WHO) says there are at least seven cases in total. Local authorities in West Africa are concerned and have not allowed the 149 passengers to disembark because they fear the disease will spread among the local population. It may only be permitted for two sick crew members to disembark to receive medical assistance. The rest of the passengers could have to sail eight more days to reach the Canary Islands.

Hantavirus is transmitted by rodents such as rats and mice. Humans can become ill if they touch the feces, saliva, or urine of these animals. Experts believe the passengers may have contracted it during land-based bird-watching excursions. A major problem is that it can take up to eight weeks for a person to show symptoms, so authorities must monitor passengers for a long time. The people on board the ship are very worried because they feel they don't have enough information and are trapped on the ship.

## 4. Grammar Analysis

### Reflexive Verbs
- *propagarse* (to spread): The disease spreads itself, requiring the reflexive pronoun *se*. Example: "la enfermedad se propague" (the disease spreads).
- *enfermarse* / *contraer* are non-reflexive here as transmission occurs through contact.

### Passive and Active Constructions
- "El hantavirus lo transmiten roedores" — Here *lo* is a neuter article for the concept of hantavirus; the verb *transmiten* is in active third-person plural form with implicit subject (rodents).

### Infinitives After Modal Verbs
- "tienen que navegar" — *tener que* + infinitive expresses obligation ("have to").
- "pueden enfermarse" — *poder* + infinitive expresses possibility ("can").

### Passive Voice with "Se"
- "no han permitido que los pasajeros bajen" — The construction uses *han permitido* (present perfect) rather than a passive *se* construction, maintaining the agent (authorities) explicit.

### Direct and Indirect Objects
- "no han permitido que los 149 pasajeros bajen" — *los 149 pasajeros* is the subject of the subordinate clause, not the object.

### Ser vs. Estar for Adjectives
- "se encuentra muy grave" — *estar* used for temporary states (condition/condition).
- "está muy preocupada" — same pattern with *estar*.

### Sequence of Tenses
- "temen que la enfermedad se propague" — Present subjunctive (*propague*) after present tense (*temen*).

## 5. Idioms and Expressions

- **a bordo de** — aboard, on board. Used to indicate position on a vehicle (ship, plane, train).
  - "a bordo del Crucero MV Hondius" — aboard the MV Hondius cruise ship

- **hasta ahora** — so far, up to now. Temporal expression indicating an action or state that has continued from a past point to the present.
  - "Hasta ahora han fallecido tres personas" — So far, three people have died

- **tener que** — to have to, must. A periphrastic construction combining *tener* with the infinitive to express obligation.
  - "El resto de los pasajeros podría tener que navegar ocho días más" — The rest of the passengers could have to sail eight more days

- **por lo tanto** — therefore, consequently. A conjunctive phrase used to introduce a logical conclusion.
  - "por lo que las autoridades deben vigilar a los pasajeros" — therefore authorities must monitor the passengers

- **un gran problema es que** — a major problem is that. Introduces a highlighted concern or issue.
  - "Un gran problema es que pueden pasar hasta ocho semanas" — A major problem is that it can take up to eight weeks

- **sentirse atrapado** — to feel trapped. Combines the reflexive verb *sentirse* (to feel) with the past participle *atrapado* (trapped).
  - "sienten que no tienen suficiente información y están atrapados" — they feel they don't have enough information and are trapped
