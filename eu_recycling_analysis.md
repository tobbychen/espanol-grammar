# Analysis of EU Recycling Article

## Raw Spanish Text

Los avances en el reciclaje de materias primas en la UE se han estancado, aumentando sólo un punto porcentual, del 11,2% al 12,2%, en la última década (2015-2024).

Este lento crecimiento hace que el objetivo de Economía Circular de la UE para 2030, del 23,2%, parezca inalcanzable. Esto se debe a que el uso de materiales primarios tiende a superar el crecimiento de los materiales secundarios, lo que limita el aumento del índice de circularidad a pesar de que se recicle más tonelaje total.

Los Países Bajos son los que obtienen mejores resultados, evitando la extracción de nuevos materiales con una tasa de casi el 33%, seguidos de Bélgica (22,7%) e Italia (casi el 22%). Por el contrario, Rumanía tiene la tasa más baja, con un 1,3%. Seis países, entre ellos Polonia (-4,2%) y Finlandia (-3,2%), experimentaron un descenso en sus tasas de reciclaje. Las autoridades de la UE subrayan que la circularidad es crucial para la transformación de la industria y para reducir la dependencia de la UE de las importaciones de materias primas críticas, sobre todo de China. Un obstáculo para avanzar más rápidamente es la falta de legislación dirigida a grandes flujos de residuos, como los mineros, en comparación con las leyes existentes para residuos específicos como los envases.

## English Translation

Progress in the recycling of raw materials in the EU has stalled, increasing only one percentage point, from 11.2% to 12.2%, in the last decade (2015-2024).

This slow growth makes the EU's Circular Economy goal for 2030, of 23.2%, seem unattainable. This is because the use of primary materials tends to outpace the growth of secondary materials, which limits the increase of the circularity rate despite more total tonnage being recycled.

The Netherlands performs the best, avoiding the extraction of new materials with a rate of almost 33%, followed by Belgium (22.7%) and Italy (almost 22%). Conversely, Romania has the lowest rate, at 1.3%. Six countries, among them Poland (-4.2%) and Finland (-3.2%), experienced a decline in their recycling rates. EU authorities stress that circularity is crucial for the industry's transformation and for reducing the EU's dependence on critical raw material imports, especially from China. One obstacle to faster progress is the lack of legislation targeting large waste streams, such as mining, in comparison with existing laws for specific wastes like packaging.

## Grammatical Analysis

*   **Present Perfect Tense (`se han estancado`)**: The text opens with this tense to describe an action (stalling) that began in the past but has a direct effect on the present situation. The reflexive pronoun `se` indicates that the "advances" themselves have stalled.
*   **Present Tense (`hace`, `tiende`, `es`, `subrayan`)**: The present indicative is used throughout to state current facts, trends, and official positions (e.g., "slow growth **makes** the goal seem unattainable," "EU authorities **stress** that...").
*   **Preterite Tense (`experimentaron`)**: The simple past (preterite) is used to describe a completed action within a specific timeframe: "Six countries **experienced** a decline." This action is finished.
*   **Subjunctive Mood (`parezca`, `se recicle`)**:
    *   `...hace que el objetivo... **parezca** inalcanzable.` The structure `hacer que` triggers the subjunctive (`parezca`) to express a resulting perception or state.
    *   `...a pesar de que **se recicle** más tonelaje...` The subjunctive is used after `a pesar de que` to present a hypothetical or concessive point that contrasts with the main clause.
*   **Gerunds (`aumentando`, `evitando`)**: These present participles function as adverbs. `...se han estancado, **aumentando** sólo un punto...` ("...have stalled, **increasing** only one point..."). `...mejores resultados, **evitando** la extracción...` ("...best results, **avoiding** the extraction..."). They provide additional information about the main verb's action.
*   **"Lo que" as a Relative Pronoun**: `...lo que limita el aumento...` ("...which limits the increase..."). Here, `lo que` refers to the entire preceding idea (the fact that primary material use outpaces secondary material growth), not just a single noun.
*   **Adjective Placement**: Adjectives consistently follow the nouns they describe, which is standard in Spanish (e.g., `lento crecimiento`, `materias primas críticas`, `grandes flujos`).
