## 1. Raw Spanish Text

Mucha gente se fija objetivos para el nuevo año, pero la mayoría los abandona al poco tiempo. Los estudios demuestran que sólo un pequeño número de personas alcanza sus propósitos.

La psicoterapeuta Lisa Palmer explica que esto suele ocurrir porque la gente intenta cambiar sus acciones sin cambiar su forma de pensar. Afirma que la mentalidad desempeña un papel clave en el éxito. Cuando la gente se cree capaz y digna, los objetivos parecen más posibles y menos estresantes.

Palmer aconseja a la gente que empiece con pasos pequeños y realistas. Los objetivos deben coincidir con los valores personales y el propósito de la vida. Explica que la motivación crece cuando la gente se siente apoyada, a veces con coaching o ayuda profesional. Se cometerán errores, pero el fracaso no significa que se haya perdido el objetivo. El progreso viene de levantarse de nuevo y aprender lo que funciona mejor la próxima vez.

También hace hincapié en la amabilidad hacia uno mismo. La gente debe aceptar los días imperfectos, centrarse en los puntos fuertes y probar nuevos métodos en lugar de repetir viejos hábitos. Palmer afirma que el pensamiento positivo, la salud, la felicidad y la presencia en la vida cotidiana facilitan la consecución del éxito a largo plazo.

## 2. English Translation

Many people set goals for the new year, but the majority abandon them after a short time. Studies show that only a small number of people achieve their purposes.

Psychotherapist Lisa Palmer explains that this usually happens because people try to change their actions without changing their way of thinking. She states that mindset plays a key role in success. When people believe themselves to be capable and worthy, goals seem more possible and less stressful.

Palmer advises people to start with small, realistic steps. Goals should align with personal values and life's purpose. She explains that motivation grows when people feel supported, sometimes with coaching or professional help. Mistakes will be made, but failure does not mean the goal is lost. Progress comes from getting up again and learning what works best next time.

She also emphasizes kindness towards oneself. People should accept imperfect days, focus on strengths, and try new methods instead of repeating old habits. Palmer states that positive thinking, health, happiness, and presence in daily life make it easier to achieve long-term success.

## 3. Grammar Analysis

The text uses clear, standard Spanish. Key grammatical features include:

*   **Reflexive Verbs:** These are verbs where the action reflects back on the subject.
    *   `se fija` (from *fijarse*): "sets for oneself."
    *   `se cree` (from *creerse*): "believes oneself to be."
    *   `se siente` (from *sentirse*): "feels."
    *   `levantarse`: "to get up / to rise."

*   **Present Tense:** The simple present tense (*presente de indicativo*) is used throughout to state general truths, facts, and expert opinions (e.g., `demuestran`, `explica`, `afirma`, `aconseja`).

*   **Passive `se`:** The construction `Se cometerán errores` ("Mistakes will be made") is an example of the passive voice using `se`. It describes an action without specifying who performs it, making the statement more general. It is combined here with the future tense.

*   **Verbal Periphrasis (soler + infinitive):** The phrase `suele ocurrir` means "usually happens." The verb `soler` is commonly used to express habitual actions.

*   **Subjunctive Mood:** The verb `aconsejar` (to advise) triggers the present subjunctive. In `aconseja a la gente que empiece...`, the verb `empiece` is in the subjunctive mood because it follows a verb of influence or recommendation.

## 4. Idioms and Expressions

The text contains several common Spanish phrases and expressions:

*   **fijar objetivos:** Literally "to fix objectives," this is the standard way to say "to set goals."
*   **al poco tiempo:** "After a short time" or "shortly after."
*   **forma de pensar:** Literally "form of thinking," it means "way of thinking" or "mindset."
*   **desempeñar un papel clave:** "To play a key role." A formal and common expression.
*   **coincidir con:** "To coincide with" or "to align with."
*   **hacer hincapié en:** "To place emphasis on" or simply "to emphasize/stress."
*   **hacia uno mismo:** "Towards oneself." Used to talk about self-directed actions or feelings.
*   **en lugar de:** "Instead of" or "in place of."
*   **a largo plazo:** "In the long term."
