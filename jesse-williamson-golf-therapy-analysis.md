# Analysis of Jesse Williamson and Golf Therapy

## 1. Original Spanish Text

Jesse Williamson es un marine estadounidense retirado que sirvió como soldado de infantería en Afghanistan. En 2009, su vehículo pisó una bomba colocada al borde de la carretera. Jesse sobrevivió, pero perdió ambas piernas tras 40 operaciones y perdió a varios amigos cercanos en la explosión.

Volver a casa fue muy duro mentalmente. Echaba de menos el ejército y recurrió a las drogas para lidiar con su trauma. Su vida cambió durante un programa de rehabilitación cuando un amigo le introdujo en el golf. Jugar al golf todos los días ayudó a Jesse a centrarse en el momento presente y a calmar sus pensamientos acelerados. Se enamoró de este deporte, se unió a un equipo para veteranos lesionados e incluso se convirtió en instructor de golf. Hoy en día, Jesse y su amigo dirigen una organización sin ánimo de lucro que ayuda a otros veteranos a recuperarse a través del golf. El famoso golfista profesional Bryson DeChambeau incluso le envió un mensaje a Jesse para elogiar su inspiración y su fortaleza. Jesse cree que el golf da a las personas un sentido de propósito y estructura. Ha demostrado que el deporte puede ser una herramienta poderosa para la sanación emocional y física.

## 2. English Translation

Jesse Williamson is a retired US Marine who served as an infantry soldier in Afghanistan. In 2009, his vehicle ran over a bomb placed at the edge of the road. Jesse survived, but lost both legs after 40 operations and lost several close friends in the explosion.

Returning home was very hard mentally. He missed the army and turned to drugs to deal with his trauma. His life changed during a rehabilitation program when a friend introduced him to golf. Playing golf every day helped Jesse focus on the present moment and calm his racing thoughts. He fell in love with this sport, joined a team for injured veterans, and even became a golf instructor. Today, Jesse and his friend run a non-profit organization that helps other veterans recover through golf. The famous professional golfer Bryson DeChambeau even sent Jesse a message to praise his inspiration and his strength. Jesse believes that golf gives people a sense of purpose and structure. He has shown that sport can be a powerful tool for emotional and physical healing.

## 4. Grammar Analysis

### Preterite Tense (Pretérito)
Used for completed past actions:
- **sirvió** (served) — 3rd person singular of *servir*
- **pisó** (ran over) — 3rd person singular of *pisar*
- **sobrevivió** (survived) — 3rd person singular of *sobrevivir*
- **perdió** (lost — two uses) — 3rd person singular of *perder*
- **recurrió** (resorted/turned to) — 3rd person singular of *recurrir*
- **cambió** (changed) — 3rd person singular of *cambiar*
- **introdujo** (introduced) — 3rd person singular of *introducir*
- **ayudó** (helped) — 3rd person singular of *ayudar*
- **se enamoró** (fell in love) — 3rd person singular reflexive of *enamorarse*
- **se unión** (joined) — 3rd person singular reflexive of *unirse*
- **se convirtió** (became) — 3rd person singular reflexive of *convertirse*
- **envió** (sent) — 3rd person singular of *enviar*

### Imperfect Tense (Imperfecto)
Used for ongoing/continuous past actions or descriptions:
- **echaba de menos** (missed — habitual/ongoing) — imperfect of *echar de menos*
- **era** implied in *fue muy duro* (was very hard) — description of a past state

### Idiom: *echar de menos*
- **echaba de menos el ejército** — "he missed the army" (lit. "he threw the army of less")
- This is a set expression meaning "to miss" someone or something, common in Spain

### Reflexive Verbs (Verbos Reflexivos)
- **se enamoró de** (fell in love with)
- **se unión a** (joined)
- **se convirtió en** (became)
- ** centrarse** (to focus — used with *ayudó a*)
- **recuperarse** (to recover)

### *Recurrir a* + Infinitive
- **recurrió a las drogas** — "he turned to drugs" / "he resorted to drugs"
- The preposition *a* is required with *recurrir*

### Por vs. Para
- **para lidiar con su trauma** — purpose ("in order to deal with")
- **a través del golf** — "through" / "via golf"

### Infinitives
- **volver a casa** — "to return home"
- **para recuperarse** — "to recover"
- **para lidiar con** — "to deal with"
- **calmar sus pensamientos** — "to calm his thoughts"
- **elogiar su inspiración** — "to praise his inspiration"
