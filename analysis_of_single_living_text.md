# Analysis of Spanish Text on Single Living

This document provides a detailed breakdown of the provided Spanish text, including a translation, glossary, and analysis of grammatical structures and idioms.

## Raw Spanish Text

Cada vez más personas viven solas y muchos adultos no tienen pareja. En Estados Unidos, la mitad de los hombres y mujeres jóvenes no viven con una pareja, y en otros países se producen cambios similares. El aumento de la vida en solitario crece porque la gente ha dejado de salir con otras personas durante algún tiempo, por ejemplo, durante la pandemia. Los adultos más jóvenes no aprenden habilidades básicas para conocer gente o gestionar los sentimientos de rechazo. La tecnología también cambia las citas. Muchas personas se conocen en línea y pueden elegir pareja según criterios como la altura, el trabajo o la educación. Algunas elecciones reducen el número de posibles parejas. Además, las mujeres tienen más libertad y educación, y quieren hombres con buenos trabajos y respeto. Muchas personas solteras disfrutan de su vida y su libertad, pero entre el 60 y el 70 % quiere tener pareja. Algunas personas se sienten solas o tristes porque no pueden conocer a la persona adecuada. Los solteros también plantean problemas sociales, como la necesidad de viviendas más pequeñas y la disminución del número de hijos. Incluso en países donde hombres y mujeres son iguales, el número de solteros sigue creciendo.

## English Translation

More and more people are living alone, and many adults do not have a partner. In the United States, half of young men and women do not live with a partner, and similar changes are occurring in other countries. The rise of solo living is growing because people have stopped going out with others for some time, for example, during the pandemic. Younger adults are not learning basic skills for meeting people or managing feelings of rejection. Technology is also changing dating. Many people meet online and can choose a partner according to criteria like height, job, or education. Some choices reduce the number of potential partners. Furthermore, women have more freedom and education, and they want men with good jobs and respect. Many single people enjoy their life and their freedom, but between 60% and 70% want to have a partner. Some people feel lonely or sad because they cannot meet the right person. Single people also pose social problems, such as the need for smaller housing and the decrease in the number of children. Even in countries where men and women are equal, the number of single people continues to grow.

## Grammatical Analysis

1.  **Passive and Reciprocal `se`:** This text shows two different uses of the pronoun `se`.
    *   **Passive `se`**: `se producen cambios similares` (similar changes are produced/occur). This construction is used to talk about something happening without specifying who or what is causing it. It's a common alternative to the `ser` + participle passive voice.
    *   **Reciprocal `se`**: `Muchas personas se conocen en línea` (Many people meet each other online). Here, `se` indicates that the action is reciprocal—people are meeting one another.

2.  **Verbal Periphrasis (Verb Phrases):** The text uses two important verb phrases that combine verbs to create a single idea.
    *   **`Dejar de` + Infinitive**: `la gente ha dejado de salir`. This means "to stop doing something." It's a very common way to express the cessation of an action.
    *   **`Seguir` + Gerund (`-ando`/`-iendo`)**: `el número de solteros sigue creciendo`. This means "to continue doing something" or "to keep on doing something." It emphasizes the ongoing nature of the action.

3.  **Adjective Agreement:** Notice the gender and number agreement of adjectives.
    *   `personas viven solas`: `solas` is feminine and plural to agree with `personas`.
    *   `habilidades básicas`: `básicas` is feminine and plural to agree with `habilidades`.
    *   `la persona adecuada`: `adecuada` is feminine and singular to agree with `persona`.
    *   `hombres y mujeres son iguales`: `iguales` is plural.

4.  **Expressing "to want":**
    *   `quiere tener pareja`: `querer` + infinitive is used to express "to want to do something."
    *   `quieren hombres con...`: `querer` + noun is used to express "to want a thing."

## Idioms and Expressions

1.  **Cada vez más:**
    *   **Literal:** "Each time more."
    *   **Figurative Meaning:** This is a fixed phrase that means "increasingly" or "more and more." It's the standard way to express this idea in Spanish.

2.  **Tener pareja:**
    *   **Literal:** "To have a partner."
    *   **Meaning:** This is the common and natural way to say someone is in a romantic relationship.

3.  **Salir con (alguien):**
    *   **Literal:** "To go out with (someone)."
    *   **Meaning:** This is the direct equivalent of the English phrasal verb for "to date" or "to be dating."

4.  **Plantar problemas:**
    *   **Literal:** "To plant problems."
    *   **Meaning:** A better translation is "to pose problems" or "to present challenges." The verb `plantear` is very useful for talking about presenting or bringing up ideas, questions, or difficulties for consideration.
