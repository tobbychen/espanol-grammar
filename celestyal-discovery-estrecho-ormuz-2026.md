# Analysis of First Commercial Ship Crossing Through Strait of Hormuz Since Conflict

## 1. Original Spanish Text

El 17 de abril de 2026, el crucero Celestyal Discovery se convirtió en el primer buque de pasajeros en atravesar con éxito el estrecho de Ormuz desde que estallara la guerra entre Irán y EE. UU. en febrero.

Esta vía navegable estratégica, por la que pasa el 20 % del suministro mundial de petróleo y gas, había quedado prácticamente cerrada debido al conflicto y al bloqueo naval de EE. UU. El estrecho está ahora abierto al tráfico comercial mientras dure el actual alto el fuego. Aunque otros barcos han empezado a zarpar de los puertos de Doha y Abu Dabi, la situación sigue siendo tensa.

Está previsto que el alto el fuego de dos semanas expire el 22 de abril, aunque el presidente Trump ha sugerido una posible prórga si continúan los avances hacia una resolución.

## 2. English Translation

On April 17, 2026, the Celestyal Discovery cruise ship became the first passenger vessel to successfully cross the Strait of Hormuz since the war between Iran and the USA broke out in February.

This strategic waterway, through which 20% of the world's oil and gas supply passes, had been practically closed due to the conflict and the US naval blockade. The strait is now open to commercial traffic for the duration of the current ceasefire. Although other ships have started to set sail from the ports of Doha and Abu Dhabi, the situation remains tense.

The two-week ceasefire is expected to expire on April 22, although President Trump has suggested a possible extension if progress toward a resolution continues.

## 4. Grammar Analysis

### "Desde que estallara" — imperfect subjunctive after "desde que"
- *estallara* is imperfect subjunctive (same as *estallase*)
- After "desde que" (since), the subjunctive is used to describe an uncertain/indefinite past event
- This is a literary/formal construction; everyday Spanish might use *estalló* (preterite)

### "Había quedado prácticamente cerrada" — pluperfect + participle
- *había quedado* = pluperfect of *quedar*
- *cerrada* = past participle agreeing with *vía navegable* (feminine)
- "Had been closed"

### "Mientras dure" — present subjunctive after "mientras"
- *mientras* = while, for as long as
- After "mientras" expressing duration in future/uncertain contexts, subjunctive: *dure*
- If "mientras" means "while" (simultaneous past actions), it can take indicative

### "Está previsto que expire" — passive construction + subjunctive
- *está previsto* = it is expected/planned
- After impersonal expressions of expectation, subjunctive follows: *expire*

### "Si continúan los avances" — indicative (not subjunctive)
- *continúan* is present indicative because the condition is presented as real/possible
- "Si" + indicative for real conditions; "si" + subjunctive only for hypothetical wishes

### "Sugerido una posible prórga" — past participle with implied "haber"
- *ha sugerido* = present perfect of *sugerir*
- *prórga* is the noun (not the verb "prorrogar")
