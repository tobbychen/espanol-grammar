# Pigeon Entanglement Rescue

## Summary
This article describes the work of Michael Cavaliere from the Wild Bird Fund, who rescues and rehabilitates injured pigeons in New York City. It focuses on the harm caused by `pie enredado`, a condition in which hair or discarded thread becomes tightly wrapped around a bird's feet. The text argues that pigeons should not be dismissed as pests and presents human intervention as both practical and morally necessary in an urban environment that creates many dangers for them.

## Grammar Notes
- `dedica su tiempo a rescatar y rehabilitar` uses `dedicarse/dedicar tiempo a + infinitive` to express habitual commitment to an activity.
- `Aunque mucha gente considera...` uses `aunque` with the indicative because the statement presents a real, accepted fact rather than a hypothetical one.
- `criaturas que luchan por sobrevivir` contains a relative clause with `que` modifying `criaturas`.
- `a los que se enfrentan` shows a relative structure with a preposition. The verb `enfrentarse a` requires `a`, so the clause keeps that preposition.
- `Esto ocurre cuando...` uses the present tense to describe a general or repeated process.
- `lo que provoca...` uses `lo que` to refer to the entire preceding situation and explain its consequence.
- `pueden ser tratados y liberados` is a passive construction with `ser + participle`.
- `podrían necesitar` uses the conditional to express possibility rather than certainty.
- `algo tan sencillo como cortar un hilo` uses `tan ... como` for comparison and emphasis.
- `que los humanos ayudemos` uses the subjunctive `ayudemos` because `es importante que` expresses value judgment or necessity.
- `no pueden valerse por sí mismos` uses the pronominal verb `valerse` plus the expression `por sí mismo` to mean self-reliance.

## Idioms and Expressions
- `pie enredado`: tangled foot; a set expression for birds whose feet are wrapped by hair or thread.
- `luchar por sobrevivir`: to struggle to survive.
- `enfrentarse a`: to face, to confront.
- `salvarles la vida`: to save their lives.
- `devolverle la vida`: literally to give life back to it/him/her; figuratively to save or restore life.
- `valerse por sí mismos`: to fend for themselves; to manage on their own.

## Raw Text
Michael Cavaliere, miembro del personal del Wild Bird Fund, dedica su tiempo a rescatar y rehabilitar palomas heridas en la ciudad de Nueva York.

Aunque mucha gente considera que las palomas son una plaga, Michael las ve como criaturas que luchan por sobrevivir en un entorno urbano difícil. Uno de los mayores peligros a los que se enfrentan es el «pie enredado». Esto ocurre cuando pelos humanos o hilos desechados se enredan fuertemente alrededor de las patas de un pájaro, lo que provoca lesiones graves, infecciones o incluso la pérdida de las patas. Michael explica que algunos pájaros tienen problemas leves y pueden ser tratados y liberados inmediatamente en la calle. Sin embargo, los casos más graves requieren una visita al veterinario, donde los pájaros podrían necesitar cirugía o anestesia para salvarles la vida. Las palomas de la ciudad se enfrentan a muchos retos, como la contaminación, el tráfico y la falta de comida. Para Michael, algo tan sencillo como cortar un hilo puede devolverle la vida a un pájaro. Él cree que es importante que los humanos ayudemos a los animales que no pueden valerse por sí mismos.

## Translation
Michael Cavaliere, a staff member at the Wild Bird Fund, dedicates his time to rescuing and rehabilitating injured pigeons in New York City.

Although many people consider pigeons a pest, Michael sees them as creatures struggling to survive in a difficult urban environment. One of the greatest dangers they face is `tangled foot`. This happens when human hair or discarded threads become tightly wrapped around a bird's feet, causing serious injuries, infections, or even the loss of the feet. Michael explains that some birds have minor problems and can be treated and released immediately on the street. However, the more serious cases require a visit to the veterinarian, where the birds may need surgery or anesthesia to save their lives. City pigeons face many challenges, such as pollution, traffic, and lack of food. For Michael, something as simple as cutting a thread can give a bird its life back. He believes it is important that humans help animals that cannot fend for themselves.
