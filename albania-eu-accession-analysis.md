# Analysis of Albania's EU Accession Ambitions

## 1. Original Spanish Text

El primer ministro albanés, Edi Rama, ha dicho que para su país no hay otra opción que entrar en la Unión Europea.

Ha descrito a los albaneses como fanáticos de la fe en la UE que no tienen ningún plan B. Aunque Albania es país candidato desde 2014, el proceso de adhesión tradicional es demasiado lento. Rama apoya una nueva propuesta de integración gradual. Esto permitiría a los países candidatos sentarse a la mesa con los miembros de la UE y disfrutar de beneficios anticipados antes de convertirse en miembros de pleno derecho.

Rama advirtió que los largos retrasos hacen que la gente pierda la confianza en la UE, lo que ayuda a las fuerzas antieuropeístas y a países como Rusia a difundir mentiras en los Balcanes Occidentales. Puso como ejemplo a Moldavia, donde la injerencia rusa casi ha vuelto a la opinión pública en contra de Europa. Por último, Rama comentó la guerra en Ucrania. Afirmó que, aunque Rusia está actuando actualmente como un enemigo al matar a personas, los líderes europeos deben iniciar conversaciones diplomáticas con Moscú para encontrar una forma de detener la guerra y lograr la paz.

## 2. English Translation

Albanian Prime Minister Edi Rama has said that for his country there is no other option than to join the European Union.

He described Albanians as fanatics of faith in the EU who have no Plan B. Although Albania has been a candidate country since 2014, the traditional accession process is too slow. Rama supports a new gradual integration proposal. This would allow candidate countries to sit at the table with EU members and enjoy anticipated benefits before becoming full members.

Rama warned that long delays cause people to lose confidence in the EU, which helps anti-European forces and countries like Russia to spread lies in the Western Balkans. He gave the example of Moldova, where Russian interference has almost turned public opinion against Europe. Finally, Rama commented on the war in Ukraine. He stated that, although Russia is currently acting as an enemy by killing people, European leaders must initiate diplomatic conversations with Moscow to find a way to stop the war and achieve peace.

## 4. Grammar Analysis

### Key Patterns in This Text

**1. Indicative vs. Subjunctive after "aunque"**
- *Aunque Albania es país candidato* — indicative (fact)
- *Aunque Rusia está actuando...* — indicative (current reality)
- The text uses indicative throughout because Rama is describing actual situations, not hypotheticals.

**2. "Hacer que" + Subjunctive**
- *los largos retrasos hacen que la gente pierda la confianza*
  "long delays cause people to lose confidence"
- After *hacer que*, the verb in the subordinate clause uses the subjunctive (*pierda*).

**3. "No hay otra opción que" + Infinitive**
- *no hay otra opción que entrar* — "there is no option but to join"
- This construction uses *que* + infinitive to express necessity.

**4. "Lo que" as a Relative Pronoun**
- *lo que ayuda a las fuerzas...* — "which helps the forces..."
- *Lo que* refers back to the entire situation (people losing confidence).

**5. Passive Construction with "volver"**
- *la injerencia rusa casi ha vuelto a la opinión pública en contra de Europa*
  "Russian interference has almost turned public opinion against Europe"
- *volver* + noun + preposition creates a change-of-state meaning.

**6. Present Participle "al matar"**
- *al matar a personas* — "by killing people"
- The construction *al + infinitive* means "upon" or "while doing" — expressing simultaneous action.

**7. Por vs. Para**
- *por la fe en la UE* — "of faith in the EU" (attribute)
- *para encontrar una forma* — "to find a way" (purpose)
- *para detener la guerra* — "to stop the war" (purpose)

**8. "Ponerse" vs. "Volverse"**
- *ha vuelto la opinión pública en contra de Europa*
  "has turned public opinion against Europe"
- *volver* in this construction means "to turn" (change direction), not "to return."

## 5. Idioms and Expressions

- **no hay otra opción que** — "there is no other option than"
  A strong statement of necessity with no alternatives.

- **plan B** — "Plan B" / "backup plan"
  Borrowed directly from English — a common expression in Spanish business and politics.

- **de pleno derecho** — "full (member)" / "with full rights"
  *De pleno derecho* means complete, with all rights and privileges: *miembro de pleno derecho* — "full member."

- **perder la confianza** — "to lose confidence"
  A common expression for declining trust in an institution or person.

- **difundir mentiras** — "to spread lies"
  *Mentiras* (lies) combined with *difundir* (to spread) is a collocation for propaganda.

- **injerencia rusa** — "Russian interference"
  *Injerencia* specifically means unauthorized or unwanted interference in internal affairs.

- **la opinión pública** — "public opinion"
  The general sentiment of the population, often used in political contexts.

- **actuar como un enemigo** — "to act as an enemy"
  A strong political statement about someone's behavior.

- **encontrar una forma de** — "to find a way to"
  A common phrase for discovering a method: *encontrar una forma de resolver* — "to find a way to resolve."

- **lograr la paz** — "to achieve peace"
  *Lograr* means to achieve or attain something through effort: *lograr la paz* — "to achieve peace."
