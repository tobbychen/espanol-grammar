# Analysis of the Article

This article discusses a video from Iran that shows a wedding where the bride and her mother are not wearing hijabs. This is significant because the father of the bride is a very important man in Iran who orders all women to wear hijabs. The article highlights the public's anger over this hypocrisy, contrasting the strict rules enforced on poor women with the freedom of the wealthy. It also draws a parallel to the case of Mahsa Amini, who died after being arrested for not wearing her hijab correctly, which sparked widespread protests and a violent crackdown by the police. The article concludes by stating that people want all women in Iran to be free.

## English Translation

A video from Iran shows a wedding. The bride and her mother are not wearing hijabs. The father of the bride is a very important man in Iran. He orders all women to wear hijabs, but his family does not do it.

Many people are angry. They say it is not fair. Poor women must follow the rules, but the rich can do what they want.

Some people remember Mahsa Amini. The police take her away because she is not wearing her hijab correctly. She dies. Many people protest. The police injure them. The people want all women in Iran to be free.

*   **"Llevar" (to wear):** The verb "llevar" is used to mean "to wear" in the context of clothing, specifically the hijab.
*   **"Deber" (must/should):** The verb "deber" is used to express obligation, as in "Las mujeres pobres deben seguir las normas" (Poor women must follow the rules).
*   **"Poder" (can/may):** The verb "poder" is used to express ability or permission, as in "las ricas pueden hacer lo que quieran" (the rich can do what they want).
*   **"Querer" (to want):** The verb "querer" is used to express desire, as in "La gente quiere que todas las mujeres de Irán sean libres" (The people want all women in Iran to be free).
*   **Subjunctive Mood:** The subjunctive mood is used to express desires, doubts, and possibilities. Examples:
    *   "que lleven" (that they wear) - used after "ordena que" (orders that).
    *   "lo que quieran" (what they want) - used after "hacer" (to do).
    *   "que todas las mujeres de Irán sean libres" (that all women in Iran be free) - used after "quiere que" (wants that).

Now, people say that the rich and powerful in Iran have one rule and the others, another.

*   **Subjunctive Mood:** The subjunctive is used to express a command or suggestion ("que sigan") and in a subordinate clause after a verb of influence ("detuviera").

# Analysis of the Third Article

## Raw Article

Un vídeo de una boda en Irán ha provocado una gran controversia política. Las imágenes muestran a la hija y a la esposa del contralmirante Ali Shamkhani, alto funcionario iraní, vestidas al estilo occidental y sin hiyab.

Esto desató la ira porque las mujeres iraníes corrientes se enfrentan a duros castigos por comportamientos similares en virtud de las estrictas leyes islámicas. A Shamkhani, que en su día dirigió el Consejo de Seguridad Nacional de Irán durante las protestas por la muerte de Mahsa Amini, se le acusa de hipocresía, al exigir modestia a los ciudadanos mientras su propia familia hace caso omiso de estas normas. El caso pone de manifiesto un profundo doble rasero dentro de la clase dirigente de Irán, donde las figuras poderosas viven libremente mientras otras sufren. Muchos iraníes criticaron a Shamkhani en Internet, but éste rechazó la reacción y culpó a Israel de filtrar el vídeo.

La historia refleja una pauta más amplia que se observa en muchos países, donde los políticos controlan las decisiones de las mujeres en público, pero permiten normas diferentes para ellos y sus familias.

## English Translation

A video of a wedding in Iran has sparked a major political controversy. The images show the daughter and wife of Rear Admiral Ali Shamkhani, a senior Iranian official, dressed in Western style and without a hijab.

This unleashed anger because ordinary Iranian women face harsh punishments for similar behaviors under strict Islamic laws. Shamkhani, who once headed Iran's National Security Council during the protests over the death of Mahsa Amini, is accused of hypocrisy for demanding modesty from citizens while his own family ignores these rules. The case highlights a deep double standard within Iran's ruling class, where powerful figures live freely while others suffer. Many Iranians criticized Shamkhani online, but he rejected the backlash and blamed Israel for leaking the video.

The story reflects a broader pattern seen in many countries, where politicians control women's decisions in public but allow different standards for themselves and their families.

## Grammar

*   **Passive Voice:** The article uses the passive voice in "se le acusa" (he is accused) and "se observa" (is observed).
*   **Conjunctions:** The article uses conjunctions like "mientras" (while), "pero" (but), and "donde" (where) to connect ideas.
*   **Prepositional Phrases:** The article uses prepositional phrases like "en virtud de" (by virtue of) and "a pesar de" (despite).
