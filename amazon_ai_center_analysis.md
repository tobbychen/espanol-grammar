## Grammatical Analysis

This text uses a mix of past, present, and future tenses to describe the project's timeline.

- **Pretérito Indefinido (Simple Past):** The verb `construyó` (built) is used to describe a specific, completed action in the past.
- **Imperfect Tense:** The verb `era` (was) is used to describe the state of the land in the past ("it was farmland"), providing background context rather than focusing on a completed action.
- **Future Tense:** Verbs like `habrá` (there will be) and `utilizará` (it will use) are used to describe what is expected to happen as the project continues.
- **"lo que":** This is a useful neuter relative pronoun that refers to an entire concept or situation. In `lo que abarata los costes`, it refers to the entire preceding clause ("The company uses its own chips instead of Nvidia's"), which is the action that lowers the costs.

## Analysis of New Article

### English Translation

Amazon opened its largest AI data center on 1,200 acres in Indiana, housing half a million AWS Tranium 2 chips to support Anthropic, an OpenAI competitor.

Just over a year ago, the area was farmland, but now there are seven operational buildings, with plans for about 30 in total. The center will use 2.2 gigawatts of electricity, enough for more than a million homes. Locals express concern about the impact on public services, water, and roads, and Amazon is funding updates and improvements. This rapid construction is not habitual, but Amazon highlights its experience in large-scale AI infrastructure. The company designs and uses its own Tranium chips instead of Nvidia GPUs, which allows for cost efficiency and great computing capacity. Anthropic benefits from this partnership, as Amazon provides scalable AI training infrastructure.

Despite criticism about overbuilding and possible inefficiencies, the project demonstrates how large technology companies can rapidly deploy customized AI hardware on an industrial scale. The center is part of a broader AI expansion across several campuses in the United States.

### Grammar Analysis

-   **Pretérito Indefinido (Simple Past):** `abrió` (opened). Used for a completed action in the past.
-   **Gerundio:** `albergando` (housing). Used to describe an ongoing action that accompanies the main verb.
-   **"Hace poco más de un año"**: "Just over a year ago." A common time expression.
-   **Imperfect Tense:** `eran` (were). Used to describe a past state or condition.
-   **Present Tense:** `hay` (there are), `expresan` (express), `está financiando` (is funding), `es` (is), `destaca` (highlights), `diseña y utiliza` (designs and uses), `permite` (allows), `se beneficia` (benefits), `proporciona` (provides), `demuestra` (demonstrates), `forma parte` (is part of). Used for current states, habitual actions, and ongoing processes.
-   **Future Tense:** `utilizará` (will use). Used for future actions.
-   **"para dar soporte a"**: "to support." "Para" (in order to) followed by an infinitive.
-   **"lo que permite"**: "which allows." "Lo que" refers to the preceding clause, acting as a relative pronoun.
-   **"a gran escala" / "a escala industrial"**: "on a large scale" / "on an industrial scale." Common phrases to express scale.
-   **"A pesar de"**: "Despite" or "in spite of." Introduces a concession.
