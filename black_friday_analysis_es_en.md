# Analysis of Black Friday Consumer Trends

This document provides an analysis, translation, and glossary for the provided Spanish text on Black Friday shopping trends.

## Original Spanish Text

Este año, los consumidores del Viernes Negro se han pasado en gran medida a los canales online, gastando hasta ahora 8.600 millones de dólares en Internet, ya que los compradores prefieren los teléfonos y los ordenadores portátiles a ir a las tiendas físicas.

Adobe Analytics espera que el total de ventas online del Viernes Negro en EE.UU. alcance casi los 11.900 millones de dólares.

Esta tendencia del gasto online se ha visto impulsada por los grandes descuentos y la extensión de las promociones a lo largo de varias semanas, lo que ha diluido la importancia del tradicional día único de compras. A pesar de las fuertes cifras de ventas, muchos consumidores siguen siendo cautos y compran con un presupuesto ajustado, expresando su preocupación por la elevada inflación y el debilitamiento del mercado laboral. La confianza de los consumidores ha decaído, lo que lleva a los compradores a ser más selectivos y evitar endeudarse. Curiosamente, el crecimiento de las ventas depende en gran medida de los hogares con mayores ingresos, que son más resistentes a las presiones económicas y están impulsando fuertes ventas en las categorías de lujo y muebles.

Durante el día también se produjo un cambio en el tráfico de las tiendas, desapareciendo las famosas aglomeraciones de primera hora de la mañana y las largas colas. La atención se centra ahora en el Ciberlunes, que se prevé que sea el mayor día de compras por Internet del año, con unas ventas que alcanzarán los 14.200 millones de dólares.

## English Translation

This year, Black Friday consumers have largely shifted to online channels, spending $8.6 billion on the internet so far, as shoppers prefer phones and laptops over going to physical stores.

Adobe Analytics expects total online sales for Black Friday in the U.S. to reach nearly $11.9 billion.

This online spending trend has been driven by big discounts and the extension of promotions over several weeks, which has diluted the importance of the traditional single shopping day. Despite the strong sales figures, many consumers remain cautious and are shopping on a tight budget, expressing concern about high inflation and a weakening labor market. Consumer confidence has fallen, leading shoppers to be more selective and avoid going into debt. Interestingly, sales growth is largely dependent on higher-income households, which are more resilient to economic pressures and are driving strong sales in luxury and furniture categories.

During the day, there was also a shift in store traffic, with the famous early morning crowds and long lines disappearing. The focus now shifts to Cyber Monday, which is predicted to be the biggest online shopping day of the year, with sales expected to reach $14.2 billion.

## Analysis and Breakdown

### Summary of Content
The text describes a significant shift in consumer behavior during Black Friday. The main points are:
1.  **Shift to Online:** Consumers are overwhelmingly choosing online shopping over physical stores.
2.  **High Sales Volume:** Online sales are reaching record highs, with projections indicating continued growth.
3.  **Cautious Consumers:** Despite high sales, the average consumer is budget-conscious due to inflation and a weak job market. They are being more selective and avoiding debt.
4.  **Role of High-Income Households:** The growth in sales is primarily driven by wealthier households who are less affected by economic pressures.
5.  **Dilution of Black Friday:** Promotions extending over weeks have made the single day of Black Friday less critical.
6.  **Focus on Cyber Monday:** Expectations are high for Cyber Monday to surpass Black Friday as the biggest online shopping day.

### Grammatical Points

*   **Present Perfect (`se han pasado`):** "have shifted." This tense is used to describe a recent past action that has a direct connection to the present.
*   **Gerund (`gastando`):** "spending." The gerund is used here to describe a simultaneous action (consumers shifted to online, *spending* money as they did so).
*   **Reflexive Passive (`se ha visto impulsada`, `se prevé`):** "has been driven," "is predicted." In Spanish, the pronoun `se` is often used with a third-person verb to create a passive voice, indicating that the subject is receiving the action without specifying who is performing it.
*   **Subjunctive Mood (`alcance`, `sea`):** "reach," "be." The subjunctive is used after expressions of expectation (`espera que... alcance`) and prediction (`se prevé que sea`). It expresses uncertainty, desire, or possibility rather than a concrete fact.
