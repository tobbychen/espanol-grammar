# Analysis of the Sturgeon Farm Text

## Raw Text (Spanish)

Una granja de esturiones en Riofrío, al sur de España, produce lo que denomina el primer caviar ecológico del mundo. El producto solo contiene huevas de esturión y sal, sin aditivos, y se elabora íntegramente en la Unión Europea. Los peces se crían en agua de manantial natural y se alimentan con verduras ecológicas y harina de pescado de origen sostenible.

Según el veterinario Ignacio Alba Alejandre, la granja no acelera el lento crecimiento de los esturiones y les permite desarrollarse de forma natural. La empresa produce alrededor de dos toneladas de caviar al año en tres variedades y exporta a países como Japón, Francia y el Reino Unido. El tipo más exclusivo puede costar hasta 8000 euros el kilo. Además, la granja vende alrededor de 15 toneladas de carne de esturión al año.

Los responsables afirman que la certificación ecológica garantiza unos altos estándares y un buen sabor. Los restaurantes españoles incluyen cada vez más el caviar en sus cartas, ya que se ha vuelto más asequible. A nivel mundial, la mayor parte del caviar procede ahora de piscifactorías chinas.

## English Translation

A sturgeon farm in Riofrío, in southern Spain, produces what it calls the world's first organic caviar. The product contains only sturgeon roe and salt, with no additives, and is produced entirely in the European Union. The fish are raised in natural spring water and fed with organic vegetables and sustainably sourced fishmeal.

According to veterinarian Ignacio Alba Alejandre, the farm does not accelerate the slow growth of the sturgeons and allows them to develop naturally. The company produces around two tons of caviar a year in three varieties and exports to countries such as Japan, France and the United Kingdom. The most exclusive type can cost up to 8,000 euros per kilo. In addition, the farm sells around 15 tons of sturgeon meat a year.

Those responsible claim that the organic certification guarantees high standards and a good taste. Spanish restaurants are increasingly including caviar on their menus, as it has become more affordable. Globally, most caviar now comes from Chinese fish farms.

## Grammar Analysis

### Verb Tenses

The text is written almost entirely in the **Present Tense (`presente`)** to describe the current, ongoing activities of the sturgeon farm. This is common when describing a business or a process.

*   `produce` (produces)
*   `denomina` (calls/denominates)
*   `contiene` (contains)
*   `acelera` (accelerates)
*   `permite` (allows)
*   `exporta` (exports)
*   `vende` (sells)
*   `afirman` (they claim)
*   `garantiza` (guarantees)
*   `incluyen` (they include)
*   `procede` (it comes from)

### Other Grammatical Structures

*   **Passive Voice with `se`**: This is a very common construction in Spanish to form the passive voice.
    *   `se elabora` (it is produced)
    *   `se crían` (they are raised)
    *   `se alimentan` (they are fed)
*   **Reflexive Verbs**:
    *   `desarrollarse` (to develop) - The `se` at the end indicates the reflexive form.
    *   `se ha vuelto` (it has become) - From the verb `volverse`, used to indicate a change.
*   **`puede costar`**: A verbal periphrasis (`poder` + infinitive) meaning "it can cost."

## Idioms and Specific Terminology

*   **`agua de manantial`**: "spring water."
*   **`harina de pescado`**: "fishmeal."
*   **`de origen sostenible`**: "sustainably sourced."
*   **`de forma natural`**: "naturally."
*   **`al año`**: "per year."
*   **`el kilo`**: "per kilo."
*   **`cada vez más`**: "more and more" or "increasingly."
*   **`A nivel mundial`**: "Globally" or "on a global level."
