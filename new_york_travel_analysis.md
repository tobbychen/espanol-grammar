# Analysis of Spanish Text on New York Travel

## 1. Original Spanish Text

Al atardecer, la mítica línea del horizonte neoyorquino se puede divisar desde la cima de este edificio ubicado frente al río Hudson. Edge es toda una experiencia, ya que el rascacielos no solo proporciona una de las mejores vistas de Nueva York, sino que cuenta con varias propuestas que sacan provecho a sus 345 m. Con esa altura, se consagra como la construcción más alta de Occidente. ¡Ten cuidado si sufres de vértigo! Parte del suelo del mirador es absolutamente transparente. Además, podrás optar por el City Climb, es decir, la escalada por el exterior del edificio. El atardecer es el mejor momento para disfrutar de la vista desde la cafetería.

Opción gratuita: el ferry de Staten Island a Nueva York te permite contemplar la Estatua de la Libertad desde un sitio privilegiado.

## 2. English Translation

At sunset, the mythical New York skyline can be seen from the top of this building located opposite the Hudson River. Edge is quite an experience, as the skyscraper not only provides one of the best views of New York, but it also has several offerings that take advantage of its 345m. At that height, it is established as the tallest building in the West. Be careful if you suffer from vertigo! Part of the lookout's floor is completely transparent. Furthermore, you will be able to opt for the City Climb, that is to say, the climb on the exterior of the building. Sunset is the best time to enjoy the view from the cafe.

Free option: the ferry from Staten Island to New York allows you to gaze at the Statue of Liberty from a prime location.

## 3. Grammar Analysis

This text uses descriptive language, relying heavily on the **Present Tense** to describe places and offer recommendations.

*   **Present Tense (Presente):** Used to describe facts, features, and general truths.
    *   `se puede divisar`: "can be seen." This is an impersonal `se` construction, common for general statements.
    *   `es`: "(it) is." Used multiple times to define or describe something (`Edge es...`, `El atardecer es...`).
    *   `proporciona`: "(it) provides."
    *   `cuenta con`: "(it) has." A common phrase meaning to be equipped with or to have.
    *   `sacan`: "(they) take." Refers to `propuestas`.
    *   `se consagra`: "it is established as." A reflexive verb used here to mean it holds the title or distinction.
    *   `sufres`: "(you) suffer." Used in a conditional clause `si sufres...`.
    *   `te permite`: "(it) allows you."

*   **Future Tense (Futuro):** Used to present an option available to the reader.
    *   `podrás optar`: "you will be able to opt." It frames the choice as a future possibility for the tourist.

*   **Imperative Mood (Imperativo):** Used to give a direct command or warning.
    *   `¡Ten cuidado!`: "Be careful!" This is the informal `tú` command form of `tener cuidado`.

*   **Other Structures:**
    *   **`no solo..., sino que...`**: "not only..., but also..." This is a common correlative conjunction used to add related information. `...el rascacielos no solo proporciona..., sino que cuenta con...`
    *   **`es decir`**: "that is to say" / "in other words." An explanatory phrase.

## 4. Idioms and Expressions

*   **al atardecer**: "at sunset" / "at dusk."
*   **línea del horizonte**: "skyline" (literally "line of the horizon").
*   **toda una experiencia**: "quite an experience."
*   **frente a**: "in front of" / "opposite."
*   **cuenta con**: "it has" / "it features."
*   **sacar provecho a**: "to take advantage of."
*   **se consagra como**: "it establishes itself as" / "it is renowned as."
*   **sufrir de vértigo**: "to suffer from vertigo."
*   **optar por**: "to opt for" / "to choose."
*   **sitio privilegiado**: "prime location" / "privileged spot."
