Su agencia informó que falleció en su casa de Los Ángeles tras una breve enfermedad. Muchos fans de Canadá, Estados Unidos y de todo el mundo compartieron su tristeza. O’Hara deja tras de sí una obra que mucha gente sigue disfrutando y recordando.

## 4. Grammar and Structures (Gramática y Estructuras)

This text provides excellent examples of how Spanish uses different past tenses to build a narrative.

*   **Present Perfect (`Pretérito Perfecto`)**: Used to announce recent news. It connects the past event to the present.
    *   **Examples**: "**Ha fallecido**" (Has passed away), "**ha sorprendido**" (has surprised).
*   **Preterite (`Pretérito Indefinido`)**: Used for specific, completed actions in the past (the main events of the story).
    *   **Examples**: "**ganó**" (she won), "**recibió**" (she received), "**informó**" (reported), "**falleció**" (passed away), "**compartieron**" (shared), "**se hizo** conocida" (she became known).
*   **Imperfect (`Pretérito Imperfecto`)**: Used for descriptions, background information, and habitual actions in the past ("what used to be").
    *   **Examples**: "**Era** famosa" (She was famous - a state of being), "...mucha gente la **consideraba**" (...many people considered her - an ongoing opinion), "La gente la **elogiaba**" (People praised her / used to praise her - a habitual action).
*   **Contrast between Preterite and Imperfect**: The text masterfully switches between these tenses. It uses the imperfect (`era`, `consideraba`) to describe her state and reputation, and the preterite (`ganó`, `recibió`) to list her specific, completed accomplishments.

## 5. Idioms and Expressions (Modismos y Expresiones)

*   **`Llena de vida`**: Literally "full of life," this is a common and direct idiom to describe someone as vibrant, lively, and energetic.
*   **`A lo largo de`**: Means "throughout" or "during the course of." It's used here to refer to her entire career: "**A lo largo de** su dilatada carrera..." (Throughout her long career...).
*   **`Dilatada carrera`**: A formal and respectful way to say "long and extensive career."
*   **`De todo el mundo`**: The standard way to say "from all over the world."
*   **`Dejar tras de sí`**: A common literary and formal expression meaning "to leave behind" (in the sense of a legacy).
    *   **Example**: "**Deja tras de sí** una obra..." (She leaves behind a body of work...).
