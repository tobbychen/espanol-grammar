- `para realizar`: "in order to conduct". The preposition `para` followed by an infinitive verb expresses purpose.
**Glossary:**

- `realizar`: to conduct, to carry out

## Idioms and Set Phrases

- **`fuego real`**: Literally "real fire," this is the standard military term for "live fire" or "live ammunition."
- **`verse seriamente debilitados`**: Literally "to see oneself seriously weakened," this reflexive construction means "to find oneself" or "to become" seriously weakened. It's used to describe a change in state.
- **`Al mismo tiempo`**: Literally "At the same time," this is a standard transitional phrase used exactly like its English counterpart.
- **`siguen existiendo`**: A common construction using the verb `seguir` + a gerund (`-iendo`/`-ando` form) to mean "to keep doing something" or that something "still" happens. Here, "still exist."
- **`aún por resolver`**: A common structure using `por` + infinitive to mean "yet to be done." For example, `la tarea está por hacer` (the task is yet to be done).
- **`en segundo plano`**: Literally "in a secondary plane," this directly translates to the idiom "in the background."
- **`suscita preocupación`**: A common collocation meaning "gives rise to concern" or "raises concern."
