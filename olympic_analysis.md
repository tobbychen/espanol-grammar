# Spanish Text Analysis: Winter Olympics Doping Scandal

## Raw Spanish Text

```spanish
Los Juegos Olímpicos de Invierno de 2026 en Italia han comenzado; sin embargo, un extraño problema ha desviado la atención de los récords y las medallas. Los funcionarios antidopaje están vigilando de cerca a los saltadores de esquí porque algunos atletas podrían estar intentando cambiar su forma corporal de una manera inusual.

Según los informes, algunos saltadores de esquí se inyectan sustancias como ácido hialurónico en los genitales para aumentarlos temporalmente. Creen que esto puede mejorar la aerodinámica y ayudarles a saltar más lejos. En el salto de esquí, la forma corporal y el equilibrio son extremadamente importantes. Incluso cambios muy pequeños pueden afectar al flujo de aire y a la distancia. Las investigaciones demuestran que un cambio de solo dos centímetros puede añadir varios metros a un salto, lo que puede decidir quién gana.

Este deporte ya tiene un historial de trampas, como la manipulación de los trajes en el Campeonato Mundial de Esquí de 2025. Ahora, a los responsables les preocupa que las inyecciones sean más difíciles de detectar, lo que suscita serias dudas sobre la imparcialidad y la confianza en los Juegos Olímpicos.
```

## English Translation

```english
The 2026 Winter Olympic Games in Italy have begun; however, a strange problem has diverted attention from records and medals. Anti-doping officials are closely monitoring ski jumpers because some athletes might be trying to change their body shape in an unusual way.

According to reports, some ski jumpers inject themselves with substances like hyaluronic acid into their genitals to temporarily increase them. They believe this can improve aerodynamics and help them jump further. In ski jumping, body shape and balance are extremely important. Even very small changes can affect airflow and distance. Research shows that a change of only two centimeters can add several meters to a jump, which can decide who wins.

This sport already has a history of cheating, such as the manipulation of suits at the 2025 Ski World Championship. Now, officials are concerned that the injections are more difficult to detect, which raises serious doubts about fairness and trust in the Olympic Games.
```

## Grammatical Analysis

### Verb Tenses and Moods
*   **Han comenzado** (Present Perfect): "have begun" - Indicates an action that started in the past and continues or has a strong relevance in the present.
*   **Ha desviado** (Present Perfect): "has diverted" - Similar to `han comenzado`, an action from the past with present consequences.
*   **Están vigilando** (Present Progressive): "are monitoring" - Describes an ongoing action happening at the present moment.
*   **Podrían estar intentando** (Conditional + Gerund): "could be trying" - Expresses a possibility or a hypothetical ongoing action in the present.
*   **Inyectan** (Present Indicative): "inject" - Describes a habitual action or a current general truth.
*   **Aumentarlos** (Infinitive + Pronoun): "to increase them" - Used to express purpose.
*   **Creen** (Present Indicative): "believe" - A current belief or opinion.
*   **Puede mejorar** (Present Indicative of `poder` + Infinitive): "can improve" - Expresses capability or possibility.
*   **Ayudarles** (Infinitive + Pronoun): "to help them" - Used to express purpose or outcome.
*   **Saltar** (Infinitive): "to jump" - The action verb following `ayudarles`.
*   **Son** (Present Indicative): "are" - Describes a current inherent quality or state.
*   **Pueden afectar** (Present Indicative of `poder` + Infinitive): "can affect" - Expresses capability or possibility.
*   **Demuestran** (Present Indicative): "show" - Indicates current evidence or findings.
*   **Puede añadir** (Present Indicative of `poder` + Infinitive): "can add" - Expresses capability or possibility.
*   **Puede decidir** (Present Indicative of `poder` + Infinitive): "can decide" - Expresses capability or possibility.
*   **Tiene** (Present Indicative): "has" - Describes a current possession or characteristic.
*   **Preocupa** (Present Indicative, Impersonal): "concerns" - An impersonal verb indicating a source of worry, often followed by a subordinate clause.
*   **Sean** (Present Subjunctive): "are" - Used in a subordinate clause after a verb of concern (`preocupa que`), indicating doubt, emotion, or subjective evaluation.
*   **Suscita** (Present Indicative): "raises" - Describes a current action or consequence.

### Noun and Adjective Agreement
Spanish nouns and adjectives consistently agree in gender and number.
*   `Juegos Olímpicos de Invierno` (Masculine Plural)
*   `extraño problema` (Masculine Singular)
*   `funcionarios antidopaje` (Masculine Plural, where "antidopaje" is an invariable adjective)
*   `saltadores de esquí` (Masculine Plural)
*   `forma corporal` (Feminine Singular)
*   `manera inusual` (Feminine Singular)
*   `sustancias como ácido hialurónico` (Feminine Plural for `sustancias`, Masculine Singular for `ácido hialurónico`)
*   `cambios muy pequeños` (Masculine Plural, adjectives `pequeños` agree)
*   `varios metros` (Masculine Plural, adjective `varios` agrees)
*   `historial de trampas` (Masculine Singular for `historial`, Feminine Plural for `trampas`)
*   `manipulación de los trajes` (Feminine Singular for `manipulación`, Masculine Plural for `trajes`)
*   `Campeonato Mundial de Esquí` (Masculine Singular)
*   `inyecciones` (Feminine Plural)
*   `más difíciles` (Feminine Plural, adjective `difíciles` agrees with `inyecciones`)
*   `serias dudas` (Feminine Plural, adjective `serias` agrees with `dudas`)
*   `imparcialidad y la confianza` (Both Feminine Singular nouns)
*   `grandes eventos` (Masculine Plural, adjective `grandes` agrees)

### Reflexive Verbs and Passive Constructions
*   **han comenzado**: While not strictly passive in this context, many verbs of starting can be used reflexively to imply a passive sense (e.g., `se han comenzado los juegos`). Here it's active for the games.
*   **ha desviado**: `desviar` can be used reflexively (`desviarse` - to deviate), but here `ha desviado` is active ("it has diverted").
*   **se inyectan**: "inject themselves" - This is a reflexive verb (`inyectarse`), indicating that the action is performed by the subject upon themselves.
*   **se cree que**: "it is believed that" - An impersonal construction (reflexive passive) used to express general belief without specifying the agent.

### Use of Prepositions
Various prepositions clarify relationships, locations, and purpose:
*   **en Italia**: "in Italy" (location)
*   **de los récords y las medallas**: "from records and medals" (separation/origin)
*   **de cerca**: "closely" (adverbial phrase)
*   **de esquí**: "ski" (modifying `saltadores` and `Campeonato Mundial`)
*   **para aumentarlos**: "to increase them" (purpose)
*   **en los genitales**: "into the genitals" (location/destination)
*   **para mejorar**: "to improve" (purpose)
*   **a saltar**: "to jump" (complement to `ayudarles`)
*   **al flujo de aire**: "to the airflow" (effect on)
*   **de solo dos centímetros**: "of only two centimeters" (quantity/measure)
*   **a un salto**: "to a jump" (addition to)
*   **de trampas**: "of cheating" (type of history)
*   **en el Campeonato Mundial**: "in the World Championship" (location)
*   **a los responsables**: personal "a" before `responsables` (direct object referring to people)
*   **sobre la imparcialidad y la confianza**: "about fairness and trust" (topic)
*   **en los Juegos Olímpicos**: "in the Olympic Games" (location)

## Glossary

*   **Juegos Olímpicos de Invierno**: Winter Olympic Games - A major international multi-sport event held every four years for sports practiced on snow and ice.
*   **extraño problema**: strange problem - An unusual or unexpected issue.
*   **ha desviado la atención**: has diverted the attention - To draw focus away from something else.
*   **récords y medallas**: records and medals - Achievements and awards in sports.
*   **funcionarios antidopaje**: anti-doping officials - Authorities responsible for enforcing anti-doping rules.
*   **vigilando de cerca**: monitoring closely - Observing something with great attention.
*   **saltadores de esquí**: ski jumpers - Athletes who compete in ski jumping.
*   **forma corporal**: body shape/form - The physical outline or structure of the body.
*   **manera inusual**: unusual way/manner - A non-standard or uncommon method.
*   **se inyectan**: they inject themselves - (From `inyectarse`) To administer an injection to oneself.
*   **sustancias**: substances - Materials with particular physical properties.
*   **ácido hialurónico**: hyaluronic acid - A substance naturally found in the body, used in this context for temporary tissue augmentation.
*   **genitales**: genitals - The external sex organs.
*   **aumentarlos temporalmente**: to temporarily increase them - To enlarge them for a limited time.
*   **mejorar la aerodinámica**: to improve aerodynamics - To make an object move through the air more efficiently.
*   **saltar más lejos**: to jump further - To achieve a greater distance in a jump.
*   **equilibrio**: balance - A state where opposing forces are equal, or physical stability.
*   **flujo de aire**: airflow - The movement of air.
*   **distancia**: distance - The extent of space between two points.
*   **demuestran**: show, demonstrate - (From `demostrar`) To prove or exhibit evidence.
*   **historial de trampas**: history of cheating - A record of past dishonest actions, especially in competition.
*   **manipulación de los trajes**: manipulation of the suits - Deliberately altering sports attire for an unfair advantage.
*   **Campeonato Mundial de Esquí**: Ski World Championship - A major international competition for skiing disciplines.
*   **responsables**: officials - People in positions of authority or duty.
*   **les preocupa que**: it concerns them that / they are concerned that - An expression of worry.
*   **inyecciones**: injections - The act of injecting a substance.
*   **más difíciles de detectar**: more difficult to detect - Harder to find or identify.
*   **suscita serias dudas**: raises serious doubts - To cause significant questions or uncertainties.
*   **imparcialidad**: impartiality, fairness - The principle of being unbiased and just.
*   **confianza**: trust, confidence - Belief in the reliability, truth, ability, or strength of someone or something.
*   **grandes eventos**: major events - Important and large-scale occurrences.
