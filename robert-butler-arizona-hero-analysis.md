# Analysis of Robert Butler Arizona Hero

## 1. Original Spanish Text

Robert Butler, un trabajador de una empresa de servicios públicos de Arizona, se convirtió en un héroe local después de salvar a un niño pequeño del intenso tráfico de la hora punta en el centro de Phoenix.

El dramático suco occurred el 19 de marzo, cuando un niño pequeño se escapó de repente de sus padres y corrió hacia una peligrosa carretera de cuatro carriles.

Un supervisor fue el primero en ver al niño, pero no pudo hacer nada porque una verja cerrada le bloqueaba el paso. Por suerte, Butler estaba conduciendo su camión cerca de allí y vio cómo se desarrollaba la emergencia. Aunque no suele circular por esa calle en concreto, logró aparcar y saltar del vehículo justo a tiempo. Corrió hacia el niño y lo agarró a solo unos metros de los coches que circulaban a toda velocidad. Un vídeo de una cámara de vigilancia muestra que un camión grande pasó por ese mismo punto solo unos segundos después.

Butler, que tiene un hijo de dos años, se emocionó mucho al hablar del rescate. Explicó que, como padre, sabe lo rápido que pueden moverse los niños. Espera que esta historia sirva de recordatorio para que todos los conductores estén alerta y presten atención a lo que les rodea.

## 2. English Translation

Robert Butler, a worker at a public services company in Arizona, became a local hero after saving a small child from heavy rush-hour traffic in downtown Phoenix.

The dramatic incident occurred on March 19, when a small child suddenly escaped from his parents and ran toward a dangerous four-lane highway.

A supervisor was the first to see the child, but could not do anything because a closed gate was blocking his way. Fortunately, Butler was driving his truck nearby and saw the emergency unfolding. Although he doesn't usually drive on that specific street, he managed to park and jump from the vehicle just in time. He ran toward the child and grabbed him just a few meters from the cars traveling at full speed. A surveillance camera video shows that a large truck passed through that same spot only a few seconds later.

Butler, who has a two-year-old son, became very emotional when talking about the rescue. He explained that, as a parent, he knows how fast children can move. He hopes this story serves as a reminder for all drivers to be alert and pay attention to their surroundings.

## 3. Glossary

- **Robert Butler** — (proper name, American)
- **un trabajador de una empresa de servicios públicos** — a worker at a public services company
  - *trabajador* (m.) = worker; feminine: *trabajadora*
  - *empresa* (f.) = company
  - *servicios públicos* (m. pl.) = public services
- **de Arizona** — from Arizona (no article with place names)
- **se convirtió en un héroe local** — became a local hero
  - *convertirse en* = to become (reflexive state change)
  - *héroe* (m.) = hero; pl. *héroes*
  - *local* (m./f. adj.) = local; pl. *locales*
- **después de salvar a un niño pequeño** — after saving a small child
  - *después de* + infinitive = after (doing something)
  - *salvar* = to save, to rescue
  - *niño pequeño* = small child; *niña* (f.)
  - note: *a* (personal a) before *un niño*
- **del intenso tráfico** — from the intense traffic
  - *intenso* (m. adj.) = intense; *intensa* (f.)
  - *tráfico* (m.) = traffic
- **de la hora punta** — of rush hour
  - *hora punta* (f.) = peak hour, rush hour
- **en el centro de Phoenix** — in downtown Phoenix
  - *centro* (m.) = center, downtown
  - *Phoenix* (proper noun, no article)
- **el dramático suco** — the dramatic incident
  - *dramático* (m. adj.) = dramatic; *dramática* (f.)
  - *suceso* (m.) = event, incident
- **ocurrió el 19 de marzo** — occurred on March 19
  - *ocurrir* → *ocurrió* (preterite, 3rd person sing.)
  - *marzo* (m.) = March
- **cuando un niño pequeño se escapó de repente** — when a small child escaped suddenly
  - *escaparse* = to escape
  - *de repente* = suddenly (adverbial phrase)
- **de sus padres** — from his/her parents
  - *padre* (m.) = father; pl. *padres* = parents
- **y corrió hacia una peligrosa carretera** — and ran toward a dangerous road
  - *correr* → *corrió* (preterite)
  - *hacia* = toward (preposition)
  - *peligroso* (m. adj.) = dangerous; *peligrosa* (f.)
  - *carretera* (f.) = road, highway
- **de cuatro carriles** — of four lanes
  - *carril* (m.) = lane; pl. *carriles*
- **un supervisor fue el primero en ver** — a supervisor was the first to see
  - *supervisor* (m.) = supervisor; feminine: *supervisora*
  - *fue* = was (preterite of *ser*)
  - *el primero en* = the first to (do something)
- **pero no pudo hacer nada** — but couldn't do anything
  - *poder* → *pudo* (preterite, 3rd person sing.)
- **porque una verja cerrada le bloqueaba el paso** — because a closed gate was blocking his way
  - *verja* (f.) = gate, fence
  - *cerrada* (f. adj.) = closed; from *cerrar*
  - *bloquear* = to block
  - *el paso* (m.) = the way, passage
  - *le* = to him (indirect object — the supervisor)
- **por suerte** — fortunately, luckily
  - *suerte* (f.) = luck
- **Butler estaba conduciendo su camión** — Butler was driving his truck
  - *conducir* → *estaba conduciendo* (imperfect progressive)
  - *camión* (m.) = truck; pl. *camiones*
- **cerca de allí** — nearby (near there)
  - *cerca de* = near
  - *allí* = there
- **y vio cómo se desarrollaba la emergencia** — and saw the emergency unfolding
  - *ver* → *vio* (preterite)
  - *cómo se desarrollaba* = how it was developing (imperfect + reflexive)
  - *emergencia* (f.) = emergency
- **aunque no suele circular por esa calle** — although he doesn't usually drive on that street
  - *aunque* = although
  - *suele* = usually does (from *soler* + infinitive)
  - *circular* = to drive (around), to circulate
  - *calle* (f.) = street
- **en concreto** — specifically, in concrete terms
- **logró aparcar y saltar del vehículo** — managed to park and jump from the vehicle
  - *lograr* = to manage to, to achieve
  - *aparcar* = to park
  - *saltar* = to jump
- **justo a tiempo** — just in time
- **corrió hacia el niño y lo agarró** — ran toward the child and grabbed him
  - *correr* → *corrió* (preterite)
  - *agarrar* → *agarró* (preterite)
- **a solo unos metros de los coches** — just a few meters from the cars
  - *metro* (m.) = meter; pl. *metros*
  - *coche* (m.) = car; pl. *coches*
- **que circulaban a toda velocidad** — that were traveling at full speed
  - *circular* = to drive, to travel (of vehicles)
  - *a toda velocidad* = at full speed
- **un vídeo de una cámara de vigilancia** — a video from a surveillance camera
  - *cámara* (f.) = camera
  - *vigilancia* (f.) = surveillance, watch
- **muestra que un camión grande pasó** — shows that a large truck passed
  - *mostrar* → *muestra* (present, 3rd person sing.)
  - *pasó* = passed (preterite of *pasar*)

  - *mismo* (m. adj.) = same; *misma* (f.)
  - *punto* (m.) = point, spot
- **solo unos segundos después** — only a few seconds later
- **que tiene un hijo de dos años** — who has a two-year-old son
  - *hijo* (m.) = son; *hija* (f.)
  - *dos años* = two years old
- **se emocionó mucho al hablar del rescate** — became very emotional when talking about the rescue
  - *emocionarse* = to become emotional
  - *al hablar* = when speaking / upon speaking (*al* + infinitive)
  - *rescate* (m.) = rescue
- **explicó que, como padre, sabe lo rápido que pueden moverse** — he explained that, as a parent, he knows how fast they can move
  - *explicar* → *explicó* (preterite)
  - *como padre* = as a parent
  - *saber* → *sabe* (present)
  - *lo rápido que* = how fast (exclamation)
  - *pueden moverse* = can move (from *poder* + *moverse*)
- **espera que esta historia sirva de recordatorio** — he hopes this story serves as a reminder
  - *esperar* = to hope, to wait
  - *sirva* = serves (subjunctive of *servir*)
  - *de recordatorio* = as a reminder
  - *recordatorio* (m.) = reminder
- **para que todos los conductores estén alerta** — so that all drivers are alert
  - *conductor* (m.) = driver; feminine: *conductora*
  - *estar alerta* = to be alert
- **y presten atención a lo que les rodea** — and pay attention to what surrounds them
  - *prestar atención* = to pay attention
  - *rodear* → *rodea* (present, 3rd person sing.)

## 4. Grammar Analysis

### State-Change Reflexive Verbs
- *convertirse en* — to become (a hero): reflexive construction for acquiring a new state
- Similar: *emocionarse* (to become emotional), *escaparse* (to escape)

### "Al" + Infinitive — Upon/When
- *al hablar del rescate* — when talking about the rescue
- *al + infinitive* expresses an action that triggers the main clause action

### "Lo + Adjective + Que" — Exclamatory
- *lo rápido que pueden moverse* — how fast they can move
- Structure: *lo + adjective/adverb + que + verb*

### Subjunctive After "Esperar Que"
- *espera que...sirva* — he hopes that... (it) serves
- *esperar* triggers subjunctive for the subordinate action (uncertain outcome)

### Imperfect vs. Pretérite
- **Preterite** (*ocurrió, corrió, vio, blockeaba, logró, pasó*): completed actions
- **Imperfect** (*suele circular, circulaban, blockeaba*): ongoing/habitual or background descriptions

### "Soler + Infinitive" — Habitual
- *no suele circular* — he doesn't usually drive (habitual action marker)

### Personal A with Direct Objects
- *ver al niño* — to see the child (person as direct object gets *a*)
- *salvar a un niño* — to save a child

### Por vs. Para
- **Por** (cause): *por suerte* (fortunately — "by luck")
- **Para** (purpose): *para que presten* (so that they pay)

### Relative Pronouns
- *que* — who/which/that (most common)
- *donde* — where: not in this text, but similar: *por donde* (through where)
- *el primero en ver* — the first to see (*en* marks the infinitive purpose)
