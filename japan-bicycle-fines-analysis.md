# Japan Bicycle Fines

## Summary
This article reports that Japanese police have started penalizing reckless cycling after a record number of accidents involving pedestrians. It explains the new nationwide law, lists several behaviors that can now lead to fines, and notes that phone use while cycling carries the highest penalty. The text also mentions public confusion about some of the rules, but presents the policy as an effort to improve safety and encourage more cautious behavior.

## Grammar Notes
- `está empezando a sancionar` uses `estar + gerundio` with `empezar a + infinitive` to emphasize that the action is beginning now and developing in the present.
- `a quienes circulan` uses a relative construction with `quienes` referring to people, introduced by `a` because they are the object of `sancionar`.
- `en los que se vieron involucrados peatones` contains a relative clause referring back to `accidentes`; `se vieron involucrados` is a passive-like expression meaning `pedestrians were involved`.
- `entró en vigor` is a fixed expression meaning `came into force`.
- `por infringir las normas` uses `por + infinitive` to express cause or reason.
- `mientras se circula` uses an impersonal `se` construction to mean `while one is riding/cycling`.
- `fue atropellada` is a passive construction with `ser + participle`.
- `espera que ... hagan` triggers the subjunctive `hagan` because `esperar que` expresses a desired outcome.
- `es posible que ... dé` also requires the subjunctive `dé` because it expresses possibility rather than certainty.
- `la primera vez` functions as a time expression meaning `the first time`.

## Idioms and Expressions
- `entrar en vigor`: to come into force.

## Raw Text
La policía japonesa está empezando a sancionar a quienes circulan en bicicleta de forma imprudente, tras un número récord de accidentes en los que se vieron involucrados peatones.

La nueva ley entró en vigor el miércoles pasado en todo Japón. Permite a los agentes poner multas por infringir las normas. Estas normas incluyen cosas tan simples como llevar un paraguas mientras se circula, llevar auriculares o no parar en los semáforos en rojo. La multa más alta, de unos 75 dólares, es por usar el móvil mientras se circula en bicicleta. El año pasado hubo más de 67 000 accidentes de bicicleta. En muchos casos, el ciclista no respetó las normas. Una víctima fue atropellada por un estudiante en bicicleta y todavía le duele el hombro.

Algunos ciclistas dicen que las nuevas normas sobre por dónde circular son confusas. Sin embargo, la policía espera que estas multas hagan que la gente tenga más cuidado. Estas normas son para todos los mayores de 16 años. Si la infracción es muy leve, es posible que la policía solo te dé una advertencia la primera vez.

## Translation
Japanese police are starting to penalize people who ride bicycles recklessly, after a record number of accidents involving pedestrians.

The new law came into force last Wednesday across all of Japan. It allows officers to issue fines for breaking the rules. These rules include things as simple as carrying an umbrella while riding, wearing headphones, or not stopping at red lights. The highest fine, about 75 dollars, is for using a mobile phone while riding a bicycle. Last year there were more than 67,000 bicycle accidents. In many cases, the cyclist did not follow the rules. One victim was run over by a student on a bicycle and still has shoulder pain.

Some cyclists say the new rules about where to ride are confusing. However, the police hope that these fines will make people more careful. These rules apply to everyone over the age of 16. If the violation is very minor, it is possible that the police will only give you a warning the first time.
