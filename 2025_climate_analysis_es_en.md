# Analysis of 2025 Climate Report Text

This document breaks down a Spanish text about global temperatures in 2025, providing an English translation, a grammar analysis, explanations of key phrases, and a glossary.

## Raw Spanish Text

Las temperaturas globales en 2025 fueron ligeramente inferiores a las de 2024 debido a La Niña, un fenómeno meteorológico natural que enfría el océano Pacífico. Aun así, 2025 se convirtió en el tercer año más cálido jamás registrado. Los últimos tres años han sido los más calurosos de la historia, lo que muestra una clara tendencia al calentamiento a largo plazo. Los científicos afirman que las temperaturas actuales se mantienen muy por encima de los niveles anteriores al uso intensivo de combustibles fósiles. Los expertos explican que este calentamiento se debe principalmente a que la gente sigue emitiendo carbono. Aunque parte del enfriamiento se debió a causas naturales, esto no impidió el aumento general. Los científicos advierten de que se producirán más récords de calor y fenómenos meteorológicos más intensos si las emisiones no se reducen rápidamente. Los incendios, las inundaciones y las olas de calor ya han afectado a muchas partes del mundo.

Los investigadores afirman que la ciencia es muy clara y no hay incertidumbre. Los gobiernos, las empresas y los individuos tienen un papel que desempeñar. Las personas pueden votar, gastar su dinero con cuidado y apoyar las energías más limpias. Reducir las emisiones pronto puede ayudar a frenar el calentamiento y proteger el planeta.

## English Translation

Global temperatures in 2025 were slightly lower than those of 2024 due to La Niña, a natural meteorological phenomenon that cools the Pacific Ocean. Even so, 2025 became the third warmest year ever recorded. The last three years have been the hottest in history, which shows a clear long-term warming trend. Scientists state that current temperatures remain well above the levels prior to the intensive use of fossil fuels. Experts explain that this warming is mainly due to people continuing to emit carbon. Although part of the cooling was due to natural causes, this did not prevent the overall increase. Scientists warn that more heat records and more intense weather events will occur if emissions are not reduced quickly. Fires, floods, and heat waves have already affected many parts of the world.

Researchers state that the science is very clear and there is no uncertainty. Governments, companies, and individuals have a role to play. People can vote, spend their money carefully, and support cleaner energies. Reducing emissions soon can help to curb the warming and protect the planet.

## Grammar Analysis

*   **`fueron`**: (Preterite of `ser` - to be). Used for a completed action in the past. "The temperatures **were**..." It's a finished fact for the year 2025.
*   **`enfría`**: (Present tense of `enfriar` - to cool). Used to describe a characteristic or a timeless action. La Niña is a phenomenon that **cools** the ocean; it's what it does.
*   **`se convirtió en`**: (Preterite of `convertirse` - to become/turn into). The reflexive `se` indicates a change of state. "It **became**..."
*   **`han sido`**: (Present Perfect of `ser` - to be). "have been". This tense connects the past action to the present. The last three years *have been* the hottest, and this fact is relevant *now*.
*   **`se mantienen`**: (Present tense of `mantenerse` - to remain/stay). The reflexive `se` indicates an ongoing state. "They **remain**..."
*   **`se debe a`**: A key impersonal phrase meaning "is due to" or "is because of". It explains the cause of something.
*   **`se producirán`**: (Future tense of `producirse` - to occur/be produced). The reflexive `se` is used to create a passive-like meaning. "More records **will occur/will be produced**."

## Key Phrases & Idioms

*   **`debido a`**: A formal way to say "due to" or "because of". It's used to link an effect with its cause.
    *   *Example:* "...inferiores a las de 2024 **debido a** La Niña." (...lower than those of 2024 **due to** La Niña.)
*   **`Aun así`**: Translates to "Even so" or "Nevertheless". It's a connector used to introduce a contrasting idea.
    *   *Example:* "**Aun así**, 2025 se convirtió en el tercer año más cálido..." (**Even so**, 2025 became the third warmest year...)
*   **`jamás registrado`**: Means "ever recorded". `Jamás` is a strong form of "never", and in this context, it emphasizes the superlative nature of the record.
*   **`muy por encima de`**: Translates to "well above" or "far above". `Por encima de` means "above", and `muy` intensifies it.
*   **`desempeñar un papel`**: The direct equivalent of the English idiom "to play a role".
    *   *Example:* "...los individuos tienen un **papel que desempeñar**." (...individuals have a **role to play**.)
*   **`gastar su dinero con cuidado`**: "to spend their money with care/carefully". A standard phrase.
*   **`frenar el calentamiento`**: "to curb/slow down the warming". `Frenar` literally means "to brake", but here it's used figuratively.
