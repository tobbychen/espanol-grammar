## Original Spanish Text

En 2025, el mundo experimentó muchos cambios positivos. Por primera vez, las energías limpias produjeron más electricidad que el carbón.

Países como China, India y muchos de Europa aumentaron la energía solar y eólica, mientras disminuía el uso del carbón. Varios estados europeos cerraron sus últimas centrales de carbón antes de lo previsto. Como resultado, la energía baja en carbono alcanzó una cuota récord de la electricidad mundial. Estos cambios demostraron que los grandes sistemas energéticos pueden cambiar en poco tiempo.

Los gobiernos también apoyaron a las personas y a las familias. Australia redujo la deuda estudiantil de millones de ciudadanos sin largos procesos. En Estados Unidos, Nuevo México ofreció guarderías gratuitas a todas las familias, lo que redujo los costes y creó nuevos puestos de trabajo. También creció la protección del medio ambiente. Una zona oceánica muy extensa recibió protección, y se iniciaron nuevos proyectos forestales en África y América. Estas acciones ayudaron tanto a la naturaleza como a la población local.

La ciencia y la salud progresaron mucho. Nuevas herramientas permitieron un seguimiento más rápido de la contaminación climática. Varias especies animales se recuperaron tras una larga protección. Los médicos aprobaron un tratamiento contra la malaria para recién nacidos y frenaron una grave enfermedad genética. El tratamiento del cáncer se hizo más rápido y sencillo. En general, 2025 demostró que las decisiones claras pueden mejorar la vida en todo el mundo.

## English Translation

In 2025, the world experienced many positive changes. For the first time, clean energy produced more electricity than coal.

Countries like China, India, and many in Europe increased their solar and wind energy, while the use of coal decreased. Several European states closed their last coal plants ahead of schedule. As a result, low-carbon energy reached a record share of global electricity. These changes demonstrated that large energy systems can change in a short amount of time.

Governments also supported individuals and families. Australia reduced the student debt of millions of citizens without lengthy processes. In the United States, New Mexico offered free daycare to all families, which reduced costs and created new jobs. Environmental protection also grew. A very extensive ocean area received protection, and new forestry projects were started in Africa and America. These actions helped both nature and the local population.

Science and health progressed greatly. New tools allowed for faster tracking of climate pollution. Several animal species recovered after long-term protection. Doctors approved a treatment for malaria for newborns and halted a serious genetic disease. Cancer treatment became faster and simpler. Overall, 2025 showed that clear decisions can improve life around the world.

## Grammar Analysis

The text primarily uses the **Pretérito Indefinido** (Simple Past or Preterite) to describe completed actions and events that occurred in 2025. This tense is for specific, finished actions in the past.

*   **Examples of Preterite:**
    *   `experimentó` (experienced)
    *   `produjeron` (produced)
    *   `aumentaron` (increased)
    *   `cerraron` (closed)
    *   `alcanzó` (reached)
    *   `demostraron` (demonstrated)
    *   `ofreció` (offered)
    *   `recibió` (received)
    *   `aprobaron` (approved)

The **Pretérito Imperfecto** (Imperfect) is used to describe ongoing background actions or situations in the past.

*   **Example of Imperfect:**
    *   `mientras disminuía el uso del carbón` (while the use of coal was decreasing). The action of 'decreasing' is presented as a continuous process happening at the same time other actions (like increasing solar) occurred.

**Other Grammatical Points:**

*   **Past Participle as Adjective:** `recién nacidos` (newborns). "Nacidos" is the past participle of the verb "nacer" (to be born) but functions here as an adjective modifying "recién" (newly) to form a compound concept.
*   **Impersonal `se`:** `se recuperaron` (they recovered). This reflexive construction is often used to describe events that seem to happen on their own or to depersonalize the action. Literally "they recovered themselves," it is translated simply as "they recovered."

## Idioms & Expressions

*   **Por primera vez**: "For the first time." A standard phrase to indicate a new occurrence.
*   **Antes de lo previsto**: "Ahead of schedule" or "earlier than expected." A common expression for when something happens sooner than planned.
*   **Como resultado**: "As a result." A connector phrase used to introduce a consequence.
*   **En poco tiempo**: "In a short time." Indicates a brief duration.
*   **Recién nacidos**: "Newborns." While a literal translation is "newly borns," it is the standard term for newborns in Spanish.
