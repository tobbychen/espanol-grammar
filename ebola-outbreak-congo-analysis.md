# Analysis of Ebola Outbreak in Democratic Republic of Congo

## 1. Original Spanish Text

La Organización Mundial de la Salud (OMS) ha elevado el nivel de riesgo para la salud pública del actual brote de ébola en la República Democrática del Congo.

El brote, causado por la rara cepa del virus conocida como «Bundibugyo», ya ha provocado 177 muertes y 750 casos sospechosos. Esta cepa concreta mata a aproximadamente un tercio de los infectados y, por el momento, no hay ninguna vacuna disponible.

El virus se está propagando rápidamente por las provincias orientales afectadas por la guerra, lo que dificulta a los equipos médicos controlar la situación. Para frenar la transmisión, las autoridades locales han prohibido las reuniones funerarias tradicionales, que a menudo implican el manejo de cadáveres infectados. Familiares enfurecidos incendiaron recientemente las tiendas de campaña de un hospital después de que los trabajadores sanitarios se negaran a entregar un cadáver debido al riesgo de contagio. La OMS ha enviado 11 toneladas de suministros médicos y 60 millones de dólares en fondos; sin embargo, los expertos advierten de que el virus se propaga rápidamente a través de las redes sociales y los viajes. Científicos de la Universidad de Oxford están desarrollando una nueva vacuna que podría comenzar los ensayos clínicos en un plazo de dos a tres meses.

## 2. English Translation

The World Health Organization (WHO) has raised the public health risk level for the current Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo.

The outbreak, caused by the rare virus strain known as "Bundibugyo," has already caused 177 deaths and 750 suspected cases. This specific strain kills approximately one-third of those infected and, at the moment, there is no vaccine available.

The virus is spreading rapidly through the eastern provinces affected by the war, making it difficult for medical teams to control the situation. To curb transmission, local authorities have banned traditional funeral gatherings, which often involve the handling of infected corpses. Angered relatives recently set fire to the tents of a hospital after healthcare workers refused to hand over a corpse due to the risk of contagion. WHO has sent 11 tons of medical supplies and $60 million in funding; however, experts warn that the virus spreads rapidly through social networks and travel. Scientists at the University of Oxford are developing a new vaccine that could begin clinical trials within two to three months.

## 4. Grammar Analysis

### Key Patterns

1. **Present Perfect with *ha elevado*** — "ha elevado el nivel de riesgo" = has raised the risk level. The *ha* + past participle construction shows an action with present relevance (the WHO has just made this decision).

2. ***Se está propagando* — present continuous passive** — "El virus se está propagando" = The virus is spreading. The *se + estar + gerund* construction indicates an ongoing action. The *se* here is part of the pronominal verb *propagarse*.

3. ***Se negaran* — imperfect subjunctive** — "después de que los trabajadores sanitarios se negaran a entregar" = after healthcare workers refused to hand over. The *después de que* + subjunctive construction indicates an action that preceded the main verb (*incendiaron*).

4. ***Sin embargo* — however** — "sin embargo, los expertos advierten" = however, experts warn. This transitional phrase introduces a contrasting idea.

5. ***Advertir de que* — to warn that** — "los expertos advierten de que el virus se propaga" = experts warn that the virus spreads. The verb *advertir* takes *de* before a dependent clause.

6. ***Podría comenzar* — could begin** — *podría* is the conditional of *poder*, indicating possibility rather than certainty.

7. ***En un plazo de dos a tres meses* — within a period of two to three months** — The phrase *en un plazo de* specifies the timeframe for the clinical trials to begin.

8. ***Por el momento* — for the moment** — "por el momento, no hay ninguna vacuna disponible" = at the moment, there is no vaccine available. Temporal adverbial phrase.

## 5. Idioms and Expressions

- **elevar el nivel de riesgo** — to raise the risk level. Formal phrasing common in institutional/health communications. *Elevar* here means to increase or raise.

- **por el momento** — for the moment, for now. Used to indicate current state without committing to future changes. *Por el momento, no hay...* = For now, there isn't...

- **frenar la transmisión** — to curb/stop transmission. *Frenar* literally means to brake; figuratively it means to stop or slow something down.

- **el manejo de cadáveres** — the handling of corpses. In this context, it refers to traditional funeral practices that involve direct contact with dead bodies, which spreads infection.

- **incendiar las tiendas de campaña** — to set fire to the tents. A dramatic action by bereaved families protesting infection control measures.

- **a través de** — through, by means of. *a través de las redes sociales y los viajes* = through social networks and travel.

- **en un plazo de** — within a period of. *en un plazo de dos a tres meses* = within a period of two to three months.
