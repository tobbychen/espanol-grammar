# Analysis of the Hockey Game Text

## Raw Text (Spanish)

El equipo masculino de hockey de Estados Unidos derrotó a Canadá por 2-1 en la prórroga y ganó su primera medalla de oro olímpica desde el famoso «Milagro sobre hielo» de 1980. Era solo la segunda vez que Estados Unidos vencía a Canadá en una final olímpica.

El partido fue intenso, con estrellas de la NHL en ambos equipos, muchos de ellos normalmente compañeros de equipo en la misma liga profesional. Canadá superó a Estados Unidos en tiros a puerta por 42-28, pero el portero estadounidense Connor Hellebuyck realizó una actuación sobresaliente. Tras el empate a 1-1 al final del tiempo reglamentario, Jack Hughes, de 24 años, marcó el gol de la victoria en la prórroga de tres contra tres. Hughes había perdido varios dientes tras recibir un golpe en la cara, pero siguió jugando. Esta victoria también supone la primera vez que los equipos de hockey masculino y femenino de Estados Unidos se proclaman campeones olímpicos en los mismos Juegos. Por su parte, Noruega terminó primera en el medallero general. Estados Unidos quedó en segundo lugar.

## English Translation

The US men's hockey team defeated Canada 2-1 in overtime to win their first Olympic gold medal since the famous "Miracle on Ice" in 1980. It was only the second time the United States had beaten Canada in an Olympic final.

The game was intense, with NHL stars on both teams, many of them normally teammates in the same professional league. Canada outshot the United States 42-28, but American goaltender Connor Hellebuyck had an outstanding performance. After a 1-1 tie at the end of regulation time, 24-year-old Jack Hughes scored the game-winning goal in three-on-three overtime. Hughes had lost several teeth after taking a blow to the face, but he continued to play. This victory also marks the first time that the US men's and women's hockey teams have been proclaimed Olympic champions in the same Games. For its part, Norway finished first in the overall medal standings. The United States came in second.

## Grammar Analysis

### Verb Tenses

The text uses a variety of past tenses to narrate the events:

*   **Preterite (`pretérito indefinido`)**: Used for completed actions in the past.
    *   `derrotó` (he defeated)
    *   `ganó` (he won)
    *   `fue` (it was)
    *   `superó` (it outshot)
    *   `realizó` (he performed)
    *   `marcó` (he scored)
    *   `terminó` (it finished)
    *   `quedó` (it came in/finished)
*   **Imperfect (`pretérito imperfecto`)**: Used for descriptions or actions in progress in the past.
    *   `Era` (It was) - describing the situation.
    *   `vencía` (was beating) - describing a past event that is not necessarily seen as completed.
*   **Pluperfect (`pretérito pluscuamperfecto`)**: Used for an action that happened before another past action.
    *   `había perdido` (he had lost) - He lost his teeth before the game ended.
*   **Present (`presente`)**: Used for the current significance of the past event.
    *   `supone` (it means/supposes)

### Other Grammatical Structures

*   **`siguió jugando`**: This is a verbal periphrasis (`seguir` + gerund) indicating the continuation of an action ("he continued playing").
*   **`se proclaman`**: The reflexive pronoun `se` is used here with `proclaman` to form a passive-like construction ("are proclaimed").

## Idioms and Specific Terminology

*   **`Milagro sobre hielo`**: "Miracle on Ice." A direct translation of the famous 1980 Olympic hockey game.
*   **`tiros a puerta`**: "shots on goal." A common sports term.
*   **`tiempo reglamentario`**: "regulation time."
*   **`gol de la victoria`**: "game-winning goal."
*   **`por su parte`**: "for its part" or "on the other hand." An expression used to shift focus to a different subject.
*   **`medallero general`**: "overall medal standings."
