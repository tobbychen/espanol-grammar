## Grammatical Analysis (in English)

This article primarily uses past tenses to narrate events, with some use of the present tense for ongoing facts.

**1. Verb Tenses:**
*   **Preterite (Pretérito Indefinido):** This is the most common tense, used for completed actions in the past. It serves as the narrative backbone of the text.
    *   `asistió` (attended)
    *   `dio` (gave/welcomed)
    *   `rechazó` (rejected/dismissed)
    *   `presentó` (introduced)
    *   `promovió` (promoted)
    *   `coincidió` (coincided)
    *   `consiguió` (secured/achieved)
    *   `recibir` (in the phrase `tras recibir` - after receiving)

*   **Imperfect (Pretérito Imperfecto):** Used to describe background states or conditions in the past.
    *   `estaba sentado` (was seated) - This describes Ronaldo's state/position during the dinner, not a completed action.

*   **Present (Presente):** Used for current facts or ongoing situations.
    *   `juega` (plays) - Stating the current fact that Ronaldo plays for Al-Nassr.
    *   `prosigue` (continues/pursues) - Describing the ongoing nature of the Vision 2030 program.
    *   `se enfrenta` (faces) - Describing Ronaldo's current situation regarding a penalty.

**2. Relative Clauses (Cláusulas de Relativo):**
The text uses relative clauses to add descriptive information without starting new sentences.
*   `...en la que el presidente...` (...in which the president...) - Adds information about the dinner.
*   `Ronaldo, que juega en el Al-Nassr...` (Ronaldo, who plays for Al-Nassr...) - Provides additional context about Ronaldo.
*   `...presidido por el príncipe...` (...chaired by the prince) - A passive past participle phrase that functions like a relative clause to describe the fund.
*   `Ronaldo, que consiguió la clasificación...` (Ronaldo, who secured the qualification...) - Adds another piece of information about Ronaldo.

**3. Gerunds (Gerundios):**
A gerund is used to show a consequence or simultaneous action.
*   `marcando el fin del largo aislamiento...` (marking the end of the long isolation...) - This indicates the consequence of the state dinner.

**4. Conjunctions and Prepositions:**
These are used to link ideas.
*   `tras` (after) - `tras el asesinato` shows sequence.
*   `mientras` (while) - `mientras Arabia Saudí prosigue` shows a simultaneous action.
*   `para` (for / in order to) - `para diversificar su economía` shows purpose.

**5. Stylistic Note:**
The final sentence about Ronaldo's qualification for the 2026 World Cup and his red card is grammatically correct but thematically disconnected from the rest of the article about the state dinner. It feels tacked on and unrelated to the main subject.
