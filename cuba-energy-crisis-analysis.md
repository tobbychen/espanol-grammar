# Analysis of Cuba's Energy Crisis

## 1. Original Spanish Text

Cuba se enfrenta a una grave crisis energética tras quedarse completamente sin gasóleo ni fuelóleo.

El ministro de Energía del país anunció que la red eléctrica nacional se encuentra en un estado crítico. La escasez se ha visto agravada por el bloqueo liderado por EE. UU., que amenaza con imponer aranceles a los países que vendan petróleo a Cuba. Como resultado, antiguos proveedores como Venezuela y México han dejado de enviar suministros, y el combustible de un petrolero ruso que llegó hace poco ya se ha agotado. Esta situación ha provocado apagones prolongados que a menudo duran hasta 22 horas al día. La gente tiene dificultades para hacer tareas básicas y, a veces, se despierta a las 2 de la madrugada durante breves momentos en los que hay electricidad para cocinar o lavar la ropa. Conservar los alimentos es casi imposible sin neveras que funcionen, y los productos básicos escasean.

En La Habana estallaron protestas poco habituales y airadas, en las que la gente bloqueó las calles con basura en llamas y golpeó ollas. Estados Unidos ha ofrecido 100 millones de dólares en ayuda humanitaria, pero solo si Cuba introduce cambios en su sistema comunista.

## 2. English Translation

Cuba faces a severe energy crisis after running out completely of diesel and fuel oil.

The country's Energy Minister announced that the national electrical grid is in a critical state. The scarcity has been aggravated by the U.S.-led blockade, which threatens to impose tariffs on countries that sell oil to Cuba. As a result, former suppliers like Venezuela and Mexico have stopped sending supplies, and the fuel from a Russian oil tanker that arrived recently has already been exhausted. This situation has caused prolonged blackouts that often last up to 22 hours a day. People struggle to perform basic tasks and sometimes wake up at 2 AM during brief moments when there is electricity to cook or wash clothes. Preserving food is almost impossible without working refrigerators, and basic products are scarce.

Unusual and angry protests erupted in Havana, where people blocked streets with burning trash and banged pots. The United States has offered 100 million dollars in humanitarian aid, but only if Cuba introduces changes to its communist system.

## 4. Grammar Analysis

**Noun Gender and Number:** Spanish gender agreement is clearly illustrated in *escasez* (feminine) with *agravada* (feminine past participle). Similarly, *apagones* (masculine plural) pairs with *prolongados* (masculine plural adjective). The article demonstrates standard patterns where adjectives and past participles must agree in gender and number with their nouns.

**Verb Tenses:**
- **Preterite tense** (*estallaron*, *golpeó*): Used for completed past actions — the protests erupted and someone hit things.
- **Present indicative** (*escasean*): Describes ongoing current state — basic products are scarce right now.
- **Present perfect** (*se ha agotado*): Shows an action with present relevance — the fuel has already been exhausted.

**Passive Construction:** The phrase *se ha agotado* uses the se-constructions common in Spanish to express passive or involuntary actions ("the fuel was exhausted/has been used up").

**Subordinate Clause:** *que Cuba introduce cambios* (that Cuba introduces changes) is a subclause introduced by *que*, expressing the condition attached to U.S. aid.

## 5. Idioms and Expressions

- **en llamas** — "on fire" or "ablaze." Literally "in flames," used here to describe burning trash blocking streets.
- **bloqueo liderado por EE. UU.** — "U.S.-led blockade." The term *bloqueo* is a key political term in Cuban discourse referring to the U.S. embargo.
- **se ha quedado sin** — "to be left without," used here to describe Cuba being completely out of diesel and fuel oil.
- **apagones prolongados** — "prolonged blackouts," a common expression during energy crises in Spanish-speaking countries.
- **a las 2 de la madrugada** — "at 2 in the morning," noting the odd hours people must wake to perform basic tasks when electricity briefly returns.
