# Analysis of Iran's World Cup Participation

## 1. Original Spanish Text

Todavía hay incertidumbre sobre la participación de Irán en el próximo Mundial de fútbol masculino que se celebrará en Estados Unidos.

La selección iraní se encuentra actualmente en Turquía ultimando los preparativos, pero los responsables están trabajando duro entre bastidores para conseguir los visados de viaje necesarios.

Según los protocolos de la FIFA, los países anfitriones están obligados a proporcionar visados a todos los equipos participantes, ya que es un requisito legal del Gobierno de EE. UU. Aunque Irán se ve afectado por estrictas prohibiciones de viaje impuestas por EE. UU., se supone que los jugadores y entrenadores están exentos de las restricciones para los eventos de la Copa del Mundo.

El presidente de EE. UU., Donald Trump, ha declarado que no le importa que Irán compita, y los responsables de la FIFA han calificado de positivas las recientes reuniones con Iran. El presidente de la FIFA ha destacado que el torneo debería ser un momento de unidad y esperanza, a pesar de las tensiones políticas actuales entre ambos países. La selección iraní espera que se alcance una solución rápida durante sus citas para los visados en la embajada de EE. UU. en Ankara.

## 2. English Translation

There is still uncertainty about Iran's participation in the next Men's World Cup to be held in the United States.

The Iranian national team is currently in Turkey finalizing preparations, but officials are working hard behind the scenes to obtain the necessary travel visas.

According to FIFA protocols, host countries are obligated to provide visas to all participating teams, as it is a legal requirement of the U.S. government. Although Iran is affected by strict travel bans imposed by the United States, players and coaches are supposed to be exempt from restrictions for World Cup events.

U.S. President Donald Trump has stated that it doesn't matter to them that Iran competes, and FIFA officials have described recent meetings with Iran as positive. The FIFA president has highlighted that the tournament should be a moment of unity and hope, despite the current political tensions between both countries. The Iranian national team hopes a solution will be reached quickly during their visa appointments at the U.S. embassy in Ankara.

## 4. Grammar Analysis

### 1. Subjunctive Trigger with "no le importa que"

The phrase **no le importa que** (it doesn't matter to them that) triggers the **subjunctive mood** in the following clause:

> *no le importa que Iran compita*

The verb *compita* is in the **present subjunctive** (3rd person singular) because the main clause expresses an attitude or judgment rather than a concrete fact. When the speaker says "it doesn't matter to them," they are expressing indifference about whether something happens or not — uncertainty that calls for subjunctive.

### 2. "Se supposed" — Passive Reflexive Construction

> *se supone que los jugadores y entrenadores están exentos*

**Se supone que** means "it is supposed that" or "supposedly." This construction uses the **passive reflexive** form (se + third person verb). It impersonalizes the subject — rather than saying "authorities suppose," it states a general assumption.

Conjugation: **se + supone** (3rd person singular of *suponer* in passive reflexive form).

### 3. "Verse afectado" — Reflexive Passive with Ser

> *Irán se ve afectado por estrictas prohibiciones de viaje*

The construction **verse afectado** uses the reflexive pronoun *se* with the verb *afectar* in passive form. This is a common construction to express "to be affected" — the doer of the action (the travel bans) is expressed with *por*.

### 4. Future/Ahead of Time Expression

> *...que se celebrará en Estados Unidos*

The verb **se celebrará** (will be held) uses the **passive voice** with *se*: *se + tercera persona singular del futuro de celebrar*. This is the **se-passive** construction, commonly used when the agent performing the action is unknown or irrelevant.

### 5. "Estar + gerundio" — Progressive Aspect

> *...los responsables están trabajando duro*

The construction **estar + gerundio** expresses an action in progress — "officials are working hard." This emphasizes the ongoing nature of the effort.

### 6. "Debería ser" — Conditional for Hedged Obligation

> *...el torneo debería ser un momento de unidad*

**Debería** is the **conditional mood** (3rd person singular of *deber*), used here to soften the statement. Rather than a direct "will be," it expresses what ought to be or should be — a gentle assertion of expected outcome.

## 5. Idioms and Expressions

### "Entre bastidores" (Behind the scenes)

Literally "between wings," this expression comes from theater terminology where the wings (bastidores) are the offstage areas. In English, we say "behind the scenes" — both expressions hide the work that happens away from public view.

Used in the article to describe the officials' secret work on visa arrangements.

### "Se supone que" (It is supposed that)

An expression indicating assumption or received information — "supposedly," "allegedly." It distances the speaker from full responsibility for the truth of the statement.

### "No le importa que" (It doesn't matter to them that)

A useful expression for expressing indifference about someone else's actions or events. The indirect object *le* (to them) indicates whose perspective matters.

### "Calificar de positivas" (To describe as positive / to rate as positive)

The construction *calificar de* + adjective means "to describe as" or "to rate as." Note that Spanish uses *de* (not English "as") to link the verb to its complement.

### "Ultimar los preparativos" (To finalize preparations)

A common expression — *ultimar* means to put the finishing touches on, to complete the final details. The noun *preparativos* (preparations) pairs naturally with this verb.
