# Analysis of the Trump and Inter Miami Visit Article

## Abstract

This document provides a grammatical analysis, including a glossary, of a Spanish article about President Donald Trump hosting the Inter Miami soccer team and Lionel Messi at the White House.

## Raw Text (Spanish)

El presidente Donald Trump recibió al equipo de fútbol Inter Miami en la Casa Blanca después de que el equipo ganara la Copa MLS 2025. El equipo ganó la final por 3-1 contra Vancouver en diciembre. La visita fue especial porque Lionel Messi, uno de los futbolistas más famosos del mundo, también acudió a la Casa Blanca.

Durante el evento, el presidente elogió al equipo y su éxito. Dijo que era un momento especial y un privilegio para él, ya que ningún presidente estadounidense había recibido a Messi en la Casa Blanca anteriormente. Trump también dijo que su hijo Barron es un gran admirador de Messi y de otro jugador famoso, Cristiano Ronaldo.

Messi y Jorge Mas, uno de los propietarios del club, le entregaron varios regalos al presidente. Le obsequiaron una camiseta del equipo con el número 47 y un balón de fútbol especial. El equipo también invitó al presidente a celebrar juntos la victoria del campeonato.

## English Translation

President Donald Trump received the Inter Miami soccer team at the White House after the team won the 2025 MLS Cup. The team won the final 3-1 against Vancouver in December. The visit was special because Lionel Messi, one of the most famous soccer players in the world, also went to the White House.

During the event, the president praised the team and its success. He said that it was a special moment and a privilege for him, since no American president had previously received Messi at the White House. Trump also said that his son Barron is a great admirer of Messi and of another famous player, Cristiano Ronaldo.

Messi and Jorge Mas, one of the club's owners, gave the president several gifts. They gave him a team jersey with the number 47 and a special soccer ball. The team also invited the president to celebrate the championship victory together.

## Grammatical Analysis

### Verb Tenses

The text is a narrative of a past event, so it predominantly uses past tenses.

*   **Preterite Tense (Pretérito Indefinido)**: This is the primary tense used to narrate the key actions of the event. Examples: `recibió` (received), `ganó` (won), `fue` (was), `acudió` (went), `elogió` (praised), `dijo` (said), `entregaron` (gave), `obsequiaron` (gifted), `invitó` (invited).
*   **Imperfect Tense (Pretérito Imperfecto)**: Used to describe states or conditions in the past. Example: `era` (it was), used to describe the moment as special.
*   **Past Perfect Tense (Pretérito Pluscuamperfecto)**: Used to describe an action that occurred before another past action. Example: `había recibido` (had received), indicating that no president had received Messi *before* this event.
*   **Past Subjunctive (Pretérito Imperfecto de Subjuntivo)**: `ganara` (won) is used after the conjunction `después de que` (after).

### Other Grammatical Structures

*   **The "Personal a"**: The preposition `a` is used before a direct object that is a person or a personified group. Example: `recibió al equipo` (he received the team).
*   **Indirect Object Pronouns**: The pronoun `le` is used to indicate the recipient of the gifts. Example: `le entregaron` (they gave to him), `Le obsequiaron` (They gifted to him).
