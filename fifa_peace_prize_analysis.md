# FIFA Peace Prize Analysis

## Raw Text (Spanish)

La FIFA, organismo rector del fútbol mundial, lanza un Premio de la Paz anual, que se entregará por primera vez el mes que viene en Washington DC.

El Presidente Gianni Infantino explicó que el premio honrará acciones excepcionales que promuevan la paz, aunque no reveló el destinatario.

El premio forma parte de la campaña Fútbol para el Mundo de la FIFA, que hace hincapié en el papel del fútbol para unir a la gente y dar esperanza. Infantino mantiene una estrecha relación con el presidente de Estados Unidos, Donald Trump, que recientemente no consiguió ganar el Premio Nobel de la Paz. Algunos observadores especulan con la posibilidad de que Trump reciba el nuevo galardón de la FIFA, aunque la organización insiste en que no puede resolver problemas geopolíticos. La FIFA también sostiene que el deporte y la política deben permanecer separados, aunque sus acciones sugieran lo contrario. El premio se anunciará durante el sorteo de la Copa Mundial, el 5 de diciembre en Washington, lo que añadirá atención mundial tanto al fútbol como a las iniciativas de paz, a pesar de la diversión del público y de las reacciones encontradas de aficionados y comentaristas.

---

## English Translation

FIFA, the governing body of world football, is launching an annual Peace Prize, which will be awarded for the first time next month in Washington DC.

President Gianni Infantino explained that the prize will honor exceptional actions that promote peace, although he did not reveal the recipient.

The prize is part of FIFA's Football for the World campaign, which emphasizes football's role in uniting people and giving hope. Infantino maintains a close relationship with the President of the United States, Donald Trump, who recently failed to win the Nobel Peace Prize. Some observers speculate about the possibility of Trump receiving FIFA's new award, although the organization insists that it cannot solve geopolitical problems. FIFA also maintains that sports and politics should remain separate, although its actions suggest otherwise. The prize will be announced during the World Cup draw, on December 5th in Washington, which will add global attention to both football and peace initiatives, despite public amusement and mixed reactions from fans and commentators.

## Grammar Analysis

1.  **`lanza`** (Present Tense)
    *   **Text:** `...lanza un Premio de la Paz anual...`
    *   **Analysis:** The present tense is used here to describe a current event, similar to a headline. It conveys immediacy, meaning "is launching."

2.  **`que se entregará`** (Relative Clause + Future Passive)
    *   **Text:** `...que se entregará por primera vez...`
    *   **Analysis:** `que` is a relative pronoun meaning "which." `se entregará` is the future tense of `entregar` (to deliver/award) used in the passive voice (`se` + verb). It literally means "which will be awarded." This structure is very common for describing what will happen to an object without naming who is performing the action.

3.  **`explicó`** (Preterite Tense)
    *   **Text:** `El Presidente Gianni Infantino explicó que...`
    *   **Analysis:** The preterite tense is used to describe a completed past action. He "explained" this at a specific point in the past.

4.  **`honrará`** (Future Tense)
    *   **Text:** `...el premio honrará acciones excepcionales...`
    *   **Analysis:** The simple future tense is used to state what "will happen." The prize "will honor" these actions.

5.  **`que promuevan`** (Subjunctive Mood)
    *   **Text:** `...acciones excepcionales que promuevan la paz...`
    *   **Analysis:** The present subjunctive `promuevan` is used because the actions are not specific, known facts but rather a desired characteristic. The prize honors any actions *that might promote* peace, expressing a quality or purpose rather than a concrete reality.

6.  **`hace hincapié en`** (Idiomatic Phrase)
    *   **Text:** `...que hace hincapié en el papel del fútbol...`
    *   **Analysis:** This is a common idiom meaning "to emphasize" or "to stress."

7.  **`sostiene que`** (Verb of Assertion)
    *   **Text:** `La FIFA también sostiene que el deporte y la política deben permanecer separados...`
    *   **Analysis:** `Sostener` here means "to maintain" or "to assert" an opinion or position.

8.  **`sugieran lo contrario`** (Subjunctive Mood)
    *   **Text:** `...aunque sus acciones sugieran lo contrario.`
    *   **Analysis:** The subjunctive `sugieran` is used after `aunque` (although) to imply doubt or a hypothetical situation from the speaker's perspective. It suggests that the actions *might suggest* the opposite, casting doubt on FIFA's official stance.

9.  **`reacciones encontradas`** (Adjective from Past Participle)
    *   **Text:** `...reacciones encontradas de aficionados y comentaristas.`
    *   **Analysis:** `Encontradas` is the feminine plural past participle of `encontrar` (to find). When used as an adjective, it means "conflicting" or "opposing." Here, it modifies `reacciones` (reactions), creating the phrase "mixed reactions."
