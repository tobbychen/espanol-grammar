# Analysis of Indonesian Sinkhole Text

This document provides a detailed analysis of the provided Spanish text about a sinkhole in Aceh, Indonesia. It includes a full translation, a sentence-by-sentence grammatical breakdown, a glossary of key terms, and an explanation of idiomatic phrases.

## Full Text and Translation

### Raw Text (Spanish)
Un enorme socavón en la regencia central de Aceh, Indonesia, se ha expandido hasta alcanzar unas tres hectáreas y amenaza el sustento de los agricultores locales.

Según las autoridades, el terreno sigue moviéndose debido a los deslizamientos de tierra y al movimiento del suelo a largo plazo. Las imágenes tomadas por drones muestran acantilados que atraviesan los campos de cultivo de la zona. Los agricultores afirman que han perdido sus tierras y que no pueden seguir plantando cultivos. Una agricultora, Sumiati, espera que el Gobierno les indemnice o les proporcione nuevas tierras, ya que su granja ha quedado destruida. Otro agricultor informó de que el borde del socavón se desplazó varios metros en solo un día.

Las autoridades afirman que el problema comenzó tras el terremoto de 2013, pero que el movimiento se agravó considerablemente tras las graves inundaciones de finales de 2025. Los expertos advierten de que el socavón podría seguir creciendo a menos que se controle adecuadamente el flujo de agua en la zona. La situación perjudica a la economía y acarrea problemas a los agricultores locales.

### Translation (English)
A huge sinkhole in the central regency of Aceh, Indonesia, has expanded to about three hectares and threatens the livelihood of local farmers.

According to authorities, the ground continues to move due to landslides and long-term soil movement. Images taken by drones show cliffs that cross through the area's farmlands. Farmers state that they have lost their lands and that they cannot continue planting crops. One farmer, Sumiati, hopes that the Government will compensate them or provide them with new land, as her farm has been destroyed. Another farmer reported that the edge of the sinkhole moved several meters in just one day.

Authorities state that the problem began after the 2013 earthquake, but that the movement worsened considerably after the severe floods at the end of 2025. Experts warn that the sinkhole could continue to grow unless the flow of water in the area is properly controlled. The situation harms the economy and brings problems for the local farmers.

## Sentence-by-Sentence Analysis

### **Sentence 1**
**Raw Text:** Un enorme socavón en la regencia central de Aceh, Indonesia, se ha expandido hasta alcanzar unas tres hectáreas y amenaza el sustento de los agricultores locales.
**Translation:** A huge sinkhole in the central regency of Aceh, Indonesia, has expanded to about three hectares and threatens the livelihood of local farmers.
**Grammatical Analysis:**
- `se ha expandido`: Present Perfect tense of the reflexive verb `expandirse` (to expand). It indicates a past action with relevance to the present.
- `hasta alcanzar`: The preposition `hasta` (until) followed by an infinitive (`alcanzar` - to reach) is used to express an outcome or limit.
- `unas tres hectáreas`: The word `unas` before a number means "about" or "approximately."
**Glossary:**
- `socavón`: sinkhole
- `regencia`: regency
- `hectáreas`: hectares
- `amenaza`: threatens
- `sustento`: livelihood, sustenance

### **Sentence 2**
**Raw Text:** Según las autoridades, el terreno sigue moviéndose debido a los deslizamientos de tierra y al movimiento del suelo a largo plazo.
**Translation:** According to authorities, the ground continues to move due to landslides and long-term soil movement.
**Grammatical Analysis:**
- `sigue moviéndose`: "continues to move." The structure `seguir` + gerund (`-iendo`/`-ando` form) indicates the continuation of an action.
- `debido a`: "due to." A prepositional phrase used to show cause.
**Glossary:**
- `terreno`: ground, land
- `deslizamientos de tierra`: landslides
- `suelo`: soil
- `a largo plazo`: long-term

### **Sentence 3**
**Raw Text:** Las imágenes tomadas por drones muestran acantilados que atraviesan los campos de cultivo de la zona.
**Translation:** Images taken by drones show cliffs that cross through the area's farmlands.
**Grammatical Analysis:**
- `tomadas por drones`: "taken by drones." `tomadas` is a past participle used as an adjective modifying `imágenes`. The feminine plural ending `-as` agrees with `imágenes`.
- `atraviesan`: 3rd person plural, present tense of `atravesar` (to cross). It agrees with the subject `acantilados`.
**Glossary:**
- `imágenes tomadas`: images taken
- `acantilados`: cliffs
- `atraviesan`: they cross
- `campos de cultivo`: farmlands, cultivated fields

### **Sentence 4**
**Raw Text:** Los agricultores afirman que han perdido sus tierras y que no pueden seguir plantando cultivos.
**Translation:** Farmers state that they have lost their lands and that they cannot continue planting crops.
**Grammatical Analysis:**
- `han perdido`: Present Perfect tense of `perder` (to lose).
- `pueden seguir plantando`: "can continue planting." A verbal phrase combining `poder` (can), `seguir` (to continue), and the gerund `plantando` (planting).
**Glossary:**
- `agricultores`: farmers
- `han perdido`: they have lost
- `cultivos`: crops

### **Sentence 5**
**Raw Text:** Una agricultora, Sumiati, espera que el Gobierno les indemnice o les proporcione nuevas tierras, ya que su granja ha quedado destruida.
**Translation:** One farmer, Sumiati, hopes that the Government will compensate them or provide them with new land, as her farm has been destroyed.
**Grammatical Analysis:**
- `espera que... les indemnice`: "hopes that... (it) compensates them." The verb `esperar que` (to hope that) triggers the subjunctive mood. `indemnice` is the present subjunctive of `indemnizar`.
- `les proporcione`: "(that it) provide them." Also in the present subjunctive, triggered by `espera que`.
- `ha quedado destruida`: "has been destroyed." The verb `quedar` can be used like `ser`/`estar` to indicate a resulting state. `destruida` is a past participle acting as an adjective.
**Glossary:**
- `espera`: she hopes
- `indemnice`: compensate (subjunctive)
- `proporcione`: provide (subjunctive)
- `granja`: farm
- `ha quedado destruida`: has been destroyed

### **Sentence 6**
**Raw Text:** Otro agricultor informó de que el borde del socavón se desplazó varios metros en solo un día.
**Translation:** Another farmer reported that the edge of the sinkhole moved several meters in just one day.
**Grammatical Analysis:**
- `informó de que`: "reported that." `informar de que` is a standard structure.
- `se desplazó`: Preterite tense of the reflexive verb `desplazarse` (to move, to shift). It describes a completed past action.
**Glossary:**
- `informó`: he reported
- `borde`: edge
- `se desplazó`: it moved, it shifted

### **Sentence 7**
**Raw Text:** Las autoridades afirman que el problema comenzó tras el terremoto de 2013, pero que el movimiento se agravó considerablemente tras las graves inundaciones de finales de 2025.
**Translation:** Authorities state that the problem began after the 2013 earthquake, but that the movement worsened considerably after the severe floods at the end of 2025.
**Grammatical Analysis:**
- `se agravó`: Preterite tense of the reflexive verb `agravarse` (to get worse, to worsen).
**Glossary:**
- `terremoto`: earthquake
- `se agravó`: it worsened
- `inundaciones`: floods

### **Sentence 8**
**Raw Text:** Los expertos advierten de que el socavón podría seguir creciendo a menos que se controle adecuadamente el flujo de agua en la zona.
**Translation:** Experts warn that the sinkhole could continue to grow unless the flow of water in the area is properly controlled.
**Grammatical Analysis:**
- `podría seguir creciendo`: "could continue to grow." A conditional phrase using `podría` (could), `seguir` (to continue), and the gerund `creciendo` (growing).
- `a menos que se controle`: "unless... is controlled." The phrase `a menos que` (unless) requires the subjunctive mood. `se controle` is the present subjunctive of the impersonal/passive `se`.
**Glossary:**
- `advierten`: they warn
- `podría`: it could
- `a menos que`: unless
- `se controle`: is controlled (subjunctive)
- `flujo`: flow

### **Sentence 9**
**Raw Text:** La situación perjudica a la economía y acarrea problemas a los agricultores locales.
**Translation:** The situation harms the economy and brings problems for the local farmers.
**Grammatical Analysis:**
- `perjudica a la economía`: "harms the economy." The verb `perjudicar` requires the "personal a" when the direct object is a specific entity, even if not a person.
- `acarrea problemas`: "brings/causes problems."
**Glossary:**
- `perjudica`: it harms
- `acarrea`: it brings, it leads to

## Idioms and Set Phrases

- **`a largo plazo`**: Literally "at long term," this is the standard way to say "in the long term" or "long-term."
- **`sigue moviéndose` / `seguir plantando` / `seguir creciendo`**: This structure of `seguir` + gerund (`-ando`/`-iendo` form) is a very common way to express the continuation of an action ("to keep doing something" or "to continue to do something").
- **`debido a`**: "due to" or "owing to." A standard way to express cause.
- **`a menos que`**: "unless." A conditional conjunction that always requires the subjunctive mood.
- **`ya que`**: "since" or "as." Used to introduce a cause or explanation.
