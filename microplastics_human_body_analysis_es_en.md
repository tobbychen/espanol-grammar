# Microplastics in the Human Body Analysis (ES-EN)

## Raw Spanish Text

El plástico ya no está solo en nuestro entorno; está dentro de nuestros cuerpos. Los microplásticos y los nanoplásticos entran en nuestro organismo a través del aire que respiramos, la piel y, sobre todo, a través de lo que comemos y bebemos.

Artículos cotidianos como las tablas de cortar de plástico, el agua embotellada y los recipientes aptos para el microondas liberan millones de partículas. Una vez dentro, estos diminutos invasores pueden atravesar las membranas celulares y depositarse en nuestros tejidos. Provocan inflamación a largo plazo porque el cuerpo no puede descomponerlos. Además del daño físico, los plásticos contienen más de 16 000 sustancias químicas. Algunas de ellas, como el BPA y los ftalatos, son los llamados disruptores endocrinos que alteran nuestras hormonas. Se relacionan con graves problemas de salud, como la obesidad, la diabetes y la disminución de la fertilidad.

Aunque no podemos eliminar fácilmente el plástico de nuestro cuerpo, podemos reducir nuestra exposición eligiendo fibras naturales, utilizando recipientes de vidrio o acero inoxidable y evitando los plásticos de un solo uso. Sin embargo, los expertos afirman que resolver este problema requerirá nuevas leyes y regulaciones globales para limitar la producción de plástico.

## English Translation

Plastic is no longer only in our environment; it is inside our bodies. Microplastics and nanoplastics enter our bodies through the air we breathe, the skin, and, above all, through what we eat and drink.

Everyday items such as plastic cutting boards, bottled water, and microwave-safe containers release millions of particles. Once inside, these tiny invaders can pass through cell membranes and settle in our tissues. They cause long-term inflammation because the body cannot break them down. In addition to the physical damage, plastics contain more than 16,000 chemical substances. Some of them, such as BPA and phthalates, are the so-called endocrine disruptors that alter our hormones. They are linked to serious health problems such as obesity, diabetes, and reduced fertility.

Although we cannot easily remove plastic from our bodies, we can reduce our exposure by choosing natural fibers, using glass or stainless steel containers, and avoiding single-use plastics. However, experts state that solving this problem will require new laws and global regulations to limit plastic production.

## Grammar Analysis (Key Structures)

1. `ya no ...; está ...`
- `ya no` means "no longer."
- The sentence contrasts two locations of plastic: `ya no está solo en nuestro entorno` and `está dentro de nuestros cuerpos`.

2. `a través de`
- Fixed prepositional phrase meaning "through" or "by way of."
- It appears twice for emphasis: `a través del aire...` and `a través de lo que comemos y bebemos`.

3. `el aire que respiramos`
- `que respiramos` is a relative clause modifying `aire`.
- Literally: "the air that we breathe."

4. `sobre todo`
- Common intensifier meaning "above all" or "especially."
- Here it highlights food and drink as the main source.

5. `aptos para`
- Standard adjective + preposition structure.
- `recipientes aptos para el microondas` means "containers suitable for the microwave" or "microwave-safe containers."

6. `Una vez dentro`
- Reduced time expression meaning "once inside."
- The noun `vez` plus an adverbial phrase creates a compact clause without repeating the subject.

7. `pueden atravesar ... y depositarse`
- `pueden` + infinitive expresses ability or possibility.
- `depositarse` is a pronominal infinitive meaning "to become deposited" or "to settle."

8. `porque el cuerpo no puede descomponerlos`
- `porque` introduces cause.
- `descomponerlos` combines the infinitive `descomponer` with the object pronoun `los`, referring to the plastics/particles.

9. `Además del daño físico`
- Fixed expression meaning "besides" or "in addition to."
- It introduces an additional argument beyond physical harm.

10. `los llamados disruptores endocrinos`
- `llamados` is a past participle used adjectivally.
- The whole phrase means "the so-called endocrine disruptors."

11. `Se relacionan con`
- Impersonal/passive-like `se` construction often used in formal writing.
- Here it means "they are linked to" or "they are associated with."

12. `podemos reducir nuestra exposición eligiendo..., utilizando... y evitando...`
- Main clause followed by three gerunds.
- These gerunds express means: "by choosing..., using..., and avoiding..."

13. `requerirá ... para limitar ...`
- Future tense `requerirá` signals a projected necessity.
- `para + infinitive` expresses purpose: the laws are needed "to limit" plastic production.

## Idioms and Common Expressions

- `ya no` = no longer
- `a través de` = through
- `sobre todo` = above all / especially
- `una vez dentro` = once inside
- `a largo plazo` = in the long term / long-term
- `además de` = besides / in addition to
- `los llamados` = the so-called
- `se relacionan con` = are linked to / are associated with
- `de un solo uso` = single-use
