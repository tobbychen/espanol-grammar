# Analysis of Spanish Text on Shivaratri in Nepal

## Original Spanish Text

Nepal celebró el Shivaratri en Katmandú, donde una gran multitud se reunió en el templo de Pashupatinath para honrar al dios Shiva. Decenas de miles de personas esperaron en largas colas para rezar, cantar y bailar. El festival reunió a la población local y a visitantes de la India que acudieron para participar en los rituales religiosos.

Durante la mayor parte del año, la marihuana es ilegal en Nepal. Sin embargo, la policía muestra una tolerancia poco habitual durante este festival debido a la tradicional relación de Shiva con el cannabis. Al otro lado del río Bagmati, hombres santos cubiertos de ceniza y muchos jóvenes devotos fumaban abiertamente en las colinas cercanas.

Nepal prohibió la marihuana en 1976 después de muchos años de venta legal. Algunos grupos han intentado cambiar la ley, pero estos esfuerzos no han prosperado. Durante este día, la tradición se vuelve más importante que el control estricto, y el país permite el consumo limitado de cannabis como parte de la celebración religiosa.

## English Translation

Nepal celebrated Shivaratri in Kathmandu, where a large crowd gathered at the Pashupatinath temple to honor the god Shiva. Tens of thousands of people waited in long lines to pray, sing, and dance. The festival brought together local people and visitors from India who came to participate in the religious rituals.

For most of the year, marijuana is illegal in Nepal. However, the police show unusual tolerance during this festival due to Shiva's traditional relationship with cannabis. On the other side of the Bagmati River, holy men covered in ash and many young devotees openly smoked on the nearby hills.

Nepal banned marijuana in 1976 after many years of legal sales. Some groups have tried to change the law, but these efforts have not succeeded. During this day, tradition becomes more important than strict control, and the country allows the limited consumption of cannabis as part of the religious celebration.

## Analysis

This text describes a cultural and religious event, contrasting the country's official laws with its traditional practices. The narrative primarily uses past tenses to describe the celebration, shifting to the present tense to explain the general context.

### Glossary of Key Terms

| Spanish | English | Meaning in Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Celebró** | Celebrated | From *celebrar*. The Preterite form. |
| **Multitud** | Crowd | A large number of people gathered together. |
| **Honrar** | To honor | To show great respect for someone or something. |
| **Rezar** | To pray | To communicate with a god. |
| **Población local** | Local population | The people who live in a particular area. |
| **Acudieron** | They came / flocked | From *acudir*. A formal way to say people went to a place. |
| **Tolerancia poco habitual** | Unusual tolerance | A level of acceptance that is not typical. |
| **Hombres santos** | Holy men | Men dedicated to a religious life; "sadhus." |
| **Cubiertos de ceniza** | Covered in ash | A ritual practice for some Hindu holy men. |
| **Devotos** | Devotees / Worshippers | People who are strongly dedicated to a religious figure. |
| **Prohibió** | Banned | From *prohibir*. The Preterite form. |
| **Consumo** | Consumption / Use | The act of using something. |

### Grammatical Observations

*   **Preterite Tense for Narration:** The first paragraph, which describes the specific event, is almost entirely in the **Preterite** tense (`celebró`, `se reunió`, `esperaron`, `reunió`, `acudieron`). This is standard for narrating completed past actions.
*   **Imperfect Tense for Description:** In the second paragraph, the Imperfect tense (`fumaban` - "they were smoking") is used. This provides a descriptive background action, painting a picture of what was happening continuously while the main events unfolded.
*   **`Para` + Infinitive for Purpose:** The text uses `para` followed by an infinitive several times to explain the purpose of an action:
    *   `...para honrar al dios Shiva.` (...to honor the god Shiva.)
    *   `...para rezar, cantar y bailar.` (...to pray, sing, and dance.)
    *   `...para participar en los rituales.` (...to participate in the rituals.)
*   **Present Tense for General Truths:** The second and third paragraphs switch to the **Present** tense (`es`, `muestra`, `prohibió`*, `han intentado`, `se vuelve`, `permite`) to state general facts about the law, the tradition, and the current situation. (*`Prohibió` is preterite, but it states a historical fact that is still true.*)

### Idioms and Key Phrases

*   **`la mayor parte del año`**: "most of the year." A common phrase to describe something that is true for the majority of the time.
*   **`sin embargo`**: "however." A connector used to introduce a contrasting idea.
*   **`al otro lado de`**: "on the other side of." Used to specify a location relative to a dividing line, like a river.
*   **`se vuelve más importante que`**: "becomes more important than." A phrase used to show a shift in priorities.
