# YouTuber Murder Alibi Analysis (ES-EN)

## Raw Spanish Text

Stephen McCullagh, un creador de contenido de YouTube, ha sido declarado culpable del asesinato de su pareja embarazada, Natalie McNally. Para ocultar el crimen, se inventó una coartada muy elaborada usando una retransmisión en directo de seis horas de un videojuego.

La noche del asesinato, tus suscriptores te vieron jugar y beber, pero el vídeo se había grabado en realidad cuatro días antes. McCullagh les dijo a los espectadores que no podía responder a los comentarios por problemas técnicos, para que no se dieran cuenta de que no era en directo. Mientras se reproducía el vídeo, fue a casa de Natalie y la atacó. Durante las seis semanas posteriores al crimen, se comportó como un novio afligido, asistió a su funeral e incluso visitó su tumba. Sin embargo, la policía acabó confiscando su ordenador y descubrió sus mentiras.

El tribunal escuchó que los celos podrían haber sido el motivo, ya que Natalie estaba pensando en romper con él. McCullagh será condenado a finales de este año.

## English Translation

Stephen McCullagh, a YouTube content creator, has been found guilty of the murder of his pregnant partner, Natalie McNally. To hide the crime, he invented a very elaborate alibi using a six-hour live stream of a video game.

On the night of the murder, his subscribers saw him playing and drinking, but the video had actually been recorded four days earlier. McCullagh told viewers that he could not respond to comments because of technical problems so that they would not realize it was not live. While the video was playing, he went to Natalie's house and attacked her. During the six weeks after the crime, he behaved like a grieving boyfriend, attended her funeral, and even visited her grave. However, the police eventually confiscated his computer and discovered his lies.

The court heard that jealousy could have been the motive, since Natalie was thinking about breaking up with him. McCullagh will be sentenced at the end of this year.

## Grammar Analysis (Key Structures)

1. `ha sido declarado culpable`
- Present perfect passive structure: `haber + sido + participio`.
- It means "has been declared/found guilty."

2. `del asesinato de su pareja embarazada`
- `de` links the noun `asesinato` to its complement.
- `embarazada` is an adjective modifying `pareja`.

3. `Para ocultar el crimen`
- `para + infinitive` expresses purpose.
- It means "in order to hide the crime."

4. `se inventó una coartada`
- Pronominal use of `inventarse` often adds the idea of making something up for oneself.
- Here it means "he made up / invented an alibi."

5. `se había grabado`
- Pluperfect passive-like/reflexive construction.
- It means "had been recorded."

6. `les dijo a los espectadores que no podía...`
- `les` is the indirect object pronoun referring to `los espectadores`.
- `decir a alguien que...` means "to tell someone that..."

7. `para que no se dieran cuenta de que...`
- `para que` introduces a purpose clause and requires the subjunctive.
- `se dieran` is the imperfect subjunctive of `darse`.
- `darse cuenta de` means "to realize."

8. `Mientras se reproducía el vídeo`
- `mientras` introduces a simultaneous action: "while."
- `se reproducía` is the imperfect, describing an ongoing background action.

9. `Durante las seis semanas posteriores al crimen`
- `durante` expresses duration.
- `posteriores a` means "following" or "subsequent to."

10. `se comportó como`
- Preterite of the pronominal verb `comportarse`.
- Means "he behaved like."

11. `acabó confiscando`
- `acabar + gerundio` can express that something finally happened after a process.
- Here: "the police ended up confiscating..."

12. `podrían haber sido`
- Conditional perfect structure.
- It expresses possibility/speculation about the past: "could have been."

13. `estaba pensando en romper con él`
- Imperfect progressive-like idea with `estar pensando`.
- `romper con alguien` means "to break up with someone."

14. `será condenado`
- Future passive construction.
- It means "will be sentenced / will be condemned."

15. `a finales de este año`
- Time expression meaning "at the end of this year."

## Idioms and Common Expressions

- `ser declarado culpable` = to be found guilty
- `ocultar el crimen` = to hide the crime
- `inventarse una coartada` = to make up an alibi
- `en directo` = live
- `darse cuenta de` = to realize
- `se comportó como` = behaved like
- `novio afligido` = grieving / distressed boyfriend
- `acabó confiscando` = ended up confiscating
- `romper con él` = to break up with him
- `a finales de este año` = at the end of this year
