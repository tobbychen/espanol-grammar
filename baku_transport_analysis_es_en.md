# Analysis of Spanish Text: Baku's Transportation Plan

## Original Spanish Text

Bakú (Azerbaiyán) está cambiando la forma en que se desplaza la gente para construir una ciudad más habitable y sostenible.

La ciudad diseña ahora calles y espacios públicos para las personas, no sólo para los coches. Nuevos carriles-bus, carriles-bici y zonas de desplazamiento compartido favorecen los desplazamientos a pie, en bicicleta y en transporte público. Estos cambios pretenden reducir el tráfico, disminuir la contaminación y facilitar los desplazamientos diarios.

Muchos residentes dicen que el nuevo sistema de transporte ahorra tiempo y dinero, y mejora su vida cotidiana. Las bicicletas y los patinetes ayudan a la gente a recorrer distancias cortas con rapidez, sobre todo cerca del centro de la ciudad. Las estaciones de tren y metro incluyen ahora aparcamientos para bicicletas, lo que ayuda a la gente a desplazarse sin problemas entre los distintos tipos de transporte.

El transporte público sigue siendo una parte clave de este plan. Bakú invierte en autobuses eléctricos y de bajas emisiones para que los desplazamientos sean más limpios y seguros. Los dirigentes de la ciudad afirman que las bicicletas por sí solas no bastan, por lo que es importante contar con servicios de autobús sólidos. Mientras Bakú se prepara para acoger un acontecimiento urbano mundial, su plan de transporte muestra cómo las ciudades pueden apoyar un futuro más ecológico.

## English Translation

Baku (Azerbaijan) is changing the way people get around to build a more livable and sustainable city.

The city now designs streets and public spaces for people, not just for cars. New bus lanes, bike lanes, and shared mobility zones encourage travel on foot, by bicycle, and on public transport. These changes aim to reduce traffic, decrease pollution, and facilitate daily commutes.

Many residents say the new transportation system saves time and money, and improves their daily life. Bicycles and scooters help people cover short distances quickly, especially near the city center. Train and metro stations now include bicycle parking, which helps people move smoothly between different types of transport.

Public transport remains a key part of this plan. Baku is investing in electric and low-emission buses to make travel cleaner and safer. City leaders state that bicycles alone are not enough, so it is important to have robust bus services. As Baku prepares to host a global urban event, its transportation plan shows how cities can support a greener future.

## Grammar Analysis

This text uses clear, journalistic Spanish, making it great for learning practical vocabulary and sentence structures.

*   **Present Progressive**: `está cambiando` (is changing). This construction (`estar` + gerund) emphasizes an action currently in progress.
*   **Reflexive Verb for General Action**: `se desplaza la gente` (how people get around). The reflexive `se` is often used to describe general actions without a specific subject, similar to the passive voice in English.
*   **Compound Nouns**: `carriles-bus` (bus lanes) and `carriles-bici` (bike lanes). Spanish sometimes uses hyphenated compounds, though it's more common to use `de` (e.g., `carril de bus`). This hyphenated form is common in modern, technical contexts.
*   **`para` + Infinitive for Purpose**: `para construir` (in order to build). This is a very common way to express the purpose or goal of an action.
*   **`Pretender` for "Aim"**: `pretenden reducir` (aim to reduce). As noted in other analyses, *pretender* means to aim or seek, not "to pretend."
*   **Idiomatic Phrase**: `sin problemas` (without problems / smoothly). This is a common idiom used to describe a fluid, easy process.
*   **`Seguir` + Gerund**: `sigue siendo` (remains / continues to be). This construction emphasizes the continuation of a state or action.
*   **`por sí solas`**: `las bicicletas por sí solas no bastan` (bicycles alone are not enough). This phrase means "by itself" or "on its own" and is used for emphasis.
