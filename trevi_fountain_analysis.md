# Spanish Text Analysis: Fontana di Trevi Regulations

## Raw Spanish Text

Roma pide ahora a los turistas que paguen una pequeña cuota para acercarse a la Fontana di Trevi. A partir del 2 de febrero de 2026, los visitantes pagarán 2 euros para bajar las escaleras cerca del agua y lanzar una moneda. La gente podrá seguir viendo la fuente de forma gratuita desde la plaza de arriba, y la vista seguirá siendo clara para todos.

La ciudad quiere que los visitantes muestren más respeto por el monumento. Los dirigentes municipales afirman que el objetivo principal es controlar las aglomeraciones, no obtener beneficios. La Fontana di Trevi suele estar demasiado concurrida y se producen problemas como los carteristas. La ciudad ya ha probado las colas y los límites de tiempo, y las autoridades afirman que este sistema ha funcionado bien. El año pasado, alrededor de diez millones de personas hicieron cola, con días muy concurridos en verano.

Las entradas solo se aplican durante las horas punta del día, mientras que el acceso a última hora de la tarde sigue siendo gratuito. El dinero ayudará a cuidar los lugares históricos y a mejorar las visitas; sin embargo, los residentes de Roma no pagan la tarifa. Los dirigentes municipales afirman que este plan favorece un mejor turismo y protege los lugares famosos para el futuro.

## English Translation

Rome is now asking tourists to pay a small fee to approach the Trevi Fountain. Starting from February 2, 2026, visitors will pay 2 euros to go down the stairs near the water and throw a coin. People will still be able to view the fountain for free from the square above, and the view will remain clear for everyone.

The city wants visitors to show more respect for the monument. City leaders state that the main objective is to control crowds, not to make a profit. The Trevi Fountain is usually too crowded, and problems like pickpockets occur. The city has already tested queues and time limits, and authorities affirm that this system has worked well. Last year, around ten million people queued, with very busy days in summer.

Tickets only apply during peak hours of the day, while late evening access remains free. The money will help take care of historical sites and improve visits; however, residents of Rome do not pay the fee. City leaders state that this plan promotes better tourism and protects famous sites for the future.

## Grammar Analysis

1.  **Present Tense:** Used predominantly to describe current policies, ongoing situations, and habitual actions: "pide" (asks), "pagarán" (will pay - future tense for an announced future event), "podrá seguir viendo" (will be able to continue seeing), "sigue siendo" (continues being), "quiere" (wants), "muestren" (show - subjunctive), "afirman" (state), "es" (is), "producen" (occur), "ha probado" (has tested - present perfect), "ha funcionado" (has worked - present perfect), "hicieron" (did/made - preterite), "se aplican" (are applied), "ayudará" (will help - future tense), "no pagan" (do not pay), "favorece" (promotes), "protege" (protects).
2.  **Present Subjunctive Mood:**
    *   "que paguen" (that they pay): Used after verbs of asking, requesting, or wanting (*pedir que*).
    *   "que los visitantes muestren": Used after verbs expressing desire or command (*querer que*).
3.  **Future Tense:** "pagarán" (they will pay), "podrá seguir viendo" (will be able to continue seeing), "seguirá siendo" (will continue being), "ayudará" (will help). Used for actions that will happen in the future.
4.  **Present Perfect (Pretérito Perfecto Compuesto):** "ha probado" (has tested), "ha funcionado" (has worked). Used for actions completed in the past but with relevance to the present.
5.  **Preterite Tense:** "hicieron cola" (they queued/made a line). Used for completed actions in the past.
6.  **Reflexive Verbs/Constructions:**
    *   "acercarse a": (to approach oneself to) to approach.
    *   "se producen problemas": (problems produce themselves) problems occur.
    *   "se aplican": (they apply themselves) are applied.
7.  **"Seguir + gerundio" construction:** "podrá seguir viendo" (will be able to *continue seeing*), "seguirá siendo" (will *continue being*). Indicates an action that continues.
8.  **"De forma gratuita":** An adverbial phrase meaning "for free" or "gratuitously."

## Idioms and Common Phrases

*   **pide a los turistas que paguen**: "asks tourists to pay". Common construction for requests/commands.
*   **acercarse a**: "to approach" (lit. to get oneself closer to).
*   **A partir del**: "Starting from the" or "As of the". Indicates a starting point in time.
*   **bajar las escaleras**: "to go down the stairs".
*   **lanzar una moneda**: "to throw a coin". Common action at the Trevi Fountain.
*   **de forma gratuita**: "for free" or "gratuitously". Adverbial phrase.
*   **plaza de arriba**: "the square above" (lit. square of above).
*   **suele estar demasiado concurrida**: "it is usually too crowded". "Soler + infinitive" indicates habitual action.
*   **se producen problemas**: "problems occur" (lit. problems produce themselves). Impersonal or passive reflexive construction.
*   **carteristas**: "pickpockets".
*   **ha probado las colas y los límites de tiempo**: "has tested queues and time limits". "Probar" can mean to test or to try.
*   **hicieron cola**: "they queued" or "they stood in line". (lit. they made queue).
*   **días muy concurridos**: "very busy days".
*   **en verano**: "in summer".
*   **entradas**: "tickets".
*   **se aplican**: "are applied". Reflexive construction.
*   **durante**: "during".
*   **horas punta**: "peak hours".
*   **acceso**: "access".
*   **última hora de la tarde**: "late evening".
*   **gratuito**: "free".
*   **ayudará a cuidar**: "will help to take care of". "Ayudar a" + infinitive.
*   **lugares históricos**: "historical sites".
*   **mejorar las visitas**: "to improve visits".
*   **sin embargo**: "however", "nevertheless".
*   **residentes**: "residents".
*   **no pagan la tarifa**: "do not pay the fee".
*   **plan**: "plan".
*   **favorece**: "promotes / favors". From "favorecer".
*   **mejor turismo**: "better tourism".
*   **protege**: "protects". From "proteger".
*   **lugares famosos**: "famous places / sites".
*   **para el futuro**: "for the future".
