# Analysis of Zelenskyy Fake Painting Disinformation

## 1. Original Spanish Text

Hace poco, aparecieron publicaciones en Internet en las que se afirmaba que el presidente Volodymyr Zelenskyy tiene colgado en su despacho un cuadro francés robado. Estas publicaciones incluían un vídeo que parecía un reportaje de la BBC.

El vídeo daba a entender que una famosa obra de Paul Cézanne, robada en Italia en marzo, estaba colgada en la pared durante una entrevista. Sin embargo, los verificadores de datos demostraron rápidamente que esta afirmación es falsa. El vídeo es un reportaje falso creado con inteligencia artificial y edición digital. Los expertos compararon las imágenes con una entrevista original de hace tres meses y descubrieron que el cuadro era diferente.

Los cuadros reales que hay en el despacho pertenecen a un artista ukrainiano llamado Andrii Chebotaru. Él confirmó que la obra representa un paisaje de su región natal en Crimea, no una obra maestra francesa robada. Este incidente forma parte de una campaña de desinformación coordinada por Rusia. Este tipo de operaciones usan noticias falsas para difundir historias negativas sobre Ucrania y sus líderes. En el pasado han aparecido afirmaciones falsas similares, como las historias sobre los refugiados ukrainianos en Francia. Estas campañas pretenden confundir al público utilizando medios manipulados y voces artificiales para que parezcan auténticas.

## 2. English Translation

Recently, posts appeared on the Internet claiming that President Volodymyr Zelenskyy has a stolen French painting hanging in his office. These posts included a video that appeared to be a BBC report.

The video implied that a famous work by Paul Cézanne, stolen in Italy in March, was hanging on the wall during an interview. However, data verifiers quickly demonstrated that this claim is false. The video is a fake report created with artificial intelligence and digital editing. Experts compared the images with an original interview from three months ago and discovered that the painting was different.

The real paintings in the office belong to a Ukrainian artist named Andrii Chebotaru. He confirmed that the work represents a landscape from his native region in Crimea, not a stolen French masterpiece. This incident is part of a disinformation campaign coordinated by Russia. These types of operations use fake news to spread negative stories about Ukraine and its leaders. In the past, similar false claims have appeared, such as stories about Ukrainian refugees in France. These campaigns aim to confuse the public using manipulated media and artificial voices to make them appear authentic.

## 4. Grammar Analysis

### "Hace" for Time Expressions
- *Hace poco* — recently
- *hace tres meses* — three months ago
- *Hace + time + que + verb* — "it has been X since..."

### Passive Voice with "Se" and "Ser"
Two passive constructions appear:
- *fueron robadas* (preterite passive with *ser*): *"una obra...robada en Italia"*
- *es falsa* (present passive with *ser*): *"esta afirmación es falsa"*

Also: *es un reportaje falso creado con IA* — "created with" (past participle as adjective)

### Impersonal "Se" for General Statements
- *se afirmaba* — "it was claimed" (se + 3rd person singular)
- *se afirmaba que* — "that it was claimed"
- Similar: *se sabe que* — "it is known that"

### "Dar a Entender" — Idiom for Implying
- *daba a entender que* — "it implied that" (imperfect)
- Not a direct translation of "give to understand"

### Imperfect vs. Preterite
- **Preterite** (*aparecieron, afirmó, demostraron, compararon, descubrieron, confirmó, pretendieron*): completed actions
- **Imperfect** (*afirmaba, parecía, estaba, incluían*): ongoing/continuous descriptions in the past

### Subjunctive After "Para Que"
- *para que parezcan auténticas* — "so that they seem authentic" (subjunctive because purpose clause with different subject than main verb)

### "Creer que" with Indicative vs. Subjunctive
- *Los verificadores demostraron que es falsa* — indicative (they demonstrated as fact)
- *Pretenden que el público entienda* — would use subjunctive if doubt were expressed

### "Este Tipo de" — Demonstrative + Noun
- *Este tipo de operaciones* — "these types of operations"
- *Este tipo de* + noun (singular or plural works)

### Por vs. Para
- **Por** (cause, exchange): *por Rusia* (by Russia — coordinated by)
- **Para** (purpose): *para difundir* (to spread), *para que parezcan* (so that)

### Present Perfect with "Haber"
- *Han aparecido* — have appeared (recent past with ongoing relevance)

### Relative Pronouns
- *que* — that/which/who
- *donde* — where (not in this text)
- *el cual / la cual* — which (formal alternative)
